They Also Served … Beyond Our Ministers Who Have We Employed? “Hire a secretary? Certainly not! There’s plenty of volunteer help available.” The year was 1917. Scottish rectitude would not see the spending of money unnecessarily. We were 24 years old and already  into our third Minister, Rev. David Christie. The size of the congregation was 1299. And we had neither a Secretary nor an Assistant Minister to help the minister of a growing, active congregation; two weekly Sunday services, a number of mid-week groups often seeking the minister to “speak at our next meeting.” And the Minister was also expected  to speak to community clubs, associations, etc., when invited. The year was 1919. A committee of Session was struck to find an assistant minister to help Dr. Christie. No suitable candidate was found. An alternative plan was to get Dr. Christie a Secretary. Dr. Christie approved. The same solution was already in use at Knox and other churches. But it appears none was hired. The year was 1920. It was reported in early January that the condition of Dr. Christie’s deteriorating health was raising concern. Several stresses including being without ministerial or secretarial assistance were taking their tole. Scottish rectitude or not,  Dr. Christie had to be given an 8 month leave of absence for Health reasons. The year was 1923. Miss Margaret MacKinnon was engaged as Church Deaconess. (We have no picture of her and no record of the length of her service.) In October a vote was taken to hire an assistant minister. The move to hire a secretary was still rejected. Good old volunteer help.
