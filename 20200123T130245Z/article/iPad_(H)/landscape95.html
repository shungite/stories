Stories of Westminster United Church & its People / Page 95
In 1927, Fosdick took over an NBC public service radio program, National Vespers. His listening audience was once estimated to be between two and a half and three million per week.. (One article ambiguously sets the figure at twenty million!) He retired from the program in 1946 at which point Bonnell took it over. Bonnell’s term on the program lasted into the 1960s. He went a step further than Fosdick with the program in that Bonnell became the originator of the concept of Dial-A-Prayer (what we referred to earlier as a boast by Fifth Avenue Presbyterian!) Those simple days now passed, TV Evangelists encourage people to call the program for help, but please have your credit card number handy. (Perhaps I exaggerate!) Both Fosdick and Bonnell freely donated their time on the program. NBC and, later, ABC freely donated the air time. Two to three million listeners every week and the station didn’t sell time to “sponsors.” What were they thinking? As I said, a simpler day. As previously mentioned Rev. George Sherwood Eddy led a travelling seminar to Russia in 1927. For Bonnell, who was part of the group, this experience impacted on the remaining years of his ministry. When Dr. Sherwood Eddy* “One of The greatest social prophets of our day” became available as a speaker the Winnipeg Y.M.C.A. and the Westminster Young Mens’ Club organized a mass meeting for men on November 19, 1933 in the afternoon. Eddy’s topic was “The Challenge of Russia”. Admission 50 cent in the Sanctuary, 35 cents in the Tivoli Theatre across the street. A second meeting was held that evening for both men and women. Eddy may have been the speaker, but Bonnell was surely the moving spirit behind the event. This, it should be noted, is the only reference I have found to our using the Tivoli as a back-up for crowd overflow. Not for a Sunday service with Bonnell as preacher, as the legend of Bonnell would have it, but for a special, advance tickets sold, public lecture. Bonnell visited Russia at least twice after his initial contact with Eddy. The last time, in 1958, he joined a group of clerics to scope out godless Communism once more.
