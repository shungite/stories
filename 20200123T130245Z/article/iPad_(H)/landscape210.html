Following a very lengthy and debilitating illness with multiple sclerosis, his wife, Charlotte, died. Mack remarried, and he and Myrtle remain two of our very good Winnipeg friends. We had a succession of staff members. When Mack retired, Jack Mathieson, who had served in North Honan, China, was with us for three years. When Doris Marshall moved to Toronto to be near her family and to do senior adult programming for the United Church, Susan Cawley became our deaconess. She was a recent graduate from Covenant College and was with us for two years. Susan married Harold Atwell from Trinidad, and when he finished his studies at the University of Manitoba, they moved to Trinidad. Glenys Hughes, also from Covenant College, succeeded Susan, and less than two years later married Rev. Harry Oussoren and moved back to Toronto. Barbara Bryant also came to us when she completed her two-year course at Covenant College, but stayed for only one year. Upon leaving Westminster, Barbara attended Seneca College in Toronto and graduated in early childhood education. She married a minister and they serve jointly on a pastoral charge in Saskatchewan. When Barbara was leaving, and Jack Mathieson retired, the board decided we should move into a new staffing arrangement and they called a relatively young ordained minister, Cecil Tiller. He seemed anxious to move west from Saint John, New Brunswick, and to undertake a role similar to the one that I had earlier done at Westminster, i.e., to be an associate minister with major responsibility for Christian education, to do as much visiting as possible, and to preach once each month. Joan McConnell, who had been a teacher before she and Barry had four children, was our Sunday school superintendent, and she became our director of Christian education.
