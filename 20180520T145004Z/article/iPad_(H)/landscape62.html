After 6 months medical leave he retuned o battle in December. By August, 1918 there was a sense among our men that they would win the War. Fleming wrote a long letter home to his parents on August 18 full of this hope. He concluded the letter with these words: Well, I have written too much already, as I may possibly have another show to write about before long, in which we take a more active part” (see Fleming’s letter at the end of this article). On August 26th Fleming was hit in the chest by a machine gun bullet and died instantly. The Christies were notified shortly thereafter of his death. Fleming’s letter didn’t reach home till September 8th. The Manitoba Free Press, as it was known at that time, published the letter for their readership , a letter full of details of the conditions our troops were fighting under, a letter which told people at home what war was all about. As I have said elsewhere, under that letter the shadow of Fleming’s death, telling us what war was is really all about. To our great relief the Armistice was signed in November. But our attention in 1917/18 was already on another catastrophe, the World Wide Flu Epidemic.. (or pandemic as it was also called.) Canada lost 60,000 soldiers in battle and 50,000 citizens due to influenza. At one point during the epidemic Westminster was closed for 8 weeks (public gatherings prohibited at the time.) The Congregation David’s ministry at Westminster was a period of impressive numbers, vitality and commitment in the congregation with its outreach programs (missions) its burgeoning Sunday School, a plethora of adult and youth groups active weekly, a solid commitment to Temperance, an openness to change with reference to the impending church union. While church records don’t frequently mention David by name by inference we have to recognize his presence, influence and leadership throughout. Our Official Board created in 1970 is replete with committees: Ministry & Personnel, Outreach, Worship, Property and Maintenance, Christian Education, Business and Management, Communications.
