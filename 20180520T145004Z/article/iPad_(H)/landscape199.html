Aiden’s daughter Liz Wijtkamp is presently an active member of Westminster Church. R.D.   — In His Own Words R.D. (Bert) Macfarlane represents a significant portion of the congregation which has always been the back bone of a successful Church — dedicated volunteers; the tireless men and women who run our programs and shoulder responsibilities for — among many reasons — the joy of serving. R.D. worked  in our Sunday School and our Athletic program for young people. He edited a newsletter for many years. He also fathered a girl who became, like himself, a leader every bit as strong as her father; Marilyn Macfarlane-Huband. Bert can tell his own story. The Annual Meeting he alludes to in the beginning took place in 1972 during the Ministry of Reid Vipond, the same year this document was written … “At the Annual Meeting of Westminster held in the Sanctuary this year Dr. Vipond was kind enough to draw attention to the fact that I had completed 53 years of activity in this church and invited the congregation to give suitable recognition. I am deeply grateful to our minister for making this reference. Fifty three years is a short period in life’s record and I have enjoyed every bit of it and am thankful that I had the opportunity of being associated with such fine people in serving the Master. When I arrived in Winnipeg in 1919 I made a habit of visiting various churches to determine what part of the city I would live in.I dropped into Westminster one September evening and was delighted to find that a Boys' Conference was being held and the speaker was Mr. Taylor Staten. He is the man who started Tuxis and Trail Rangers — a great friend of mine. He spotted me in the congregation and told the Conference the story of three lads from Calgary who came to the Lake of The Woods in 1910 to a Boys’ Conference.
