Lorraine Thompson and The Orphans of Oradea     For Lorraine, the culmination of a long held dream began in March and April 2005, when she went to Romania to look after orphan babies! Orphan babies, you say? Where did that come from? She never did talk about her motivation, but the answer seemed obvious. Almost all of her nursing career had been spent working in nurseries in Japan, Vancouver, Winnipeg, and for a short time in Vernon. She loved taking care of babies and had developed a great skill in doing that. Romania, you say? Actually, this dream started for Lorraine with no thought of Romania. For ages, she had talked about going somewhere, where she could care for orphan babies — the obvious choice seemed to be Africa, where Aids was causing so many orphans, but trying to find a place who would take volunteer workers for a short term became more of a challenge than you might have thought. The other obstacle had been, that she had been trying to enlist Bob on this new adventure, but with no idea of where she was going or for how long she would be there, Bob was reluctant. He had other thing he wanted to do with his retirement.
