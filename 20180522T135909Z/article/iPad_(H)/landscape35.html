1. THE CONGREGATION On October 24, 1892, the original 18 people who branched off from St. Andrew’s Elgin to found Westminster met at the office of barrister Colin Campbell to discuss the establishment of a new Presbyterian church. But before anything could be done to enact their plans, they required permission from the Presbytery. The eminent station that most these 18 occupied in Winnipeg society rendered such procedures almost a formality, and the Presbytery formally accepted their petition on November 16th, only a day after receiving it. And like that, Westminster Church (then Westminster Presbyterian Church) was born. With Reverend Professor A.B. Baird of Manitoba College acting as Interim Moderator (temporary minister and chief administrator), the new congregation met for the first time on December 7th and had their first worship service January 1st 1893 in Victoria Hall  — only six weeks after the Congress at Campbell’s. 2. THE PASTOR A permanent minister was wanting, and so the congregation met on February 1st to consider the “advisability of calling a pastor.” Pitblado’s reputation as an powerful and erudite preacher evidently still resonated with these former members of St. Andrew’s, since he was their first pick. The call was signed by 64 members and 7 adherents, and was then submitted to Presbytery, accepted, and forwarded to Pitblado in California. 3.
