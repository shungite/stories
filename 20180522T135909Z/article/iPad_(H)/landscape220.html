As an appendix to this chapter I shall include my second-last sermon at Westminster, which I entitled, “The First Forty Years are the Hardest.” There will not be much overlapping with what has been written thus far, but perhaps it gives a kind of summary. (Let me confess here that ten years later, in 1989, when I was completing my second post-retirement job, at Metropolitan in London, I used almost exactly the same sermon, simply changing the title to “The First Fifty Years are the Hardest.”) But just before the Appendix, I must relate what appeared in the Westminster Church calendar on my last Sunday, and this actually found its way into a September 1979 issue of PUNCH magazine in England. I had given the church secretary the general outline for the calendar material, including hymns, lessons, sermon subject, etc. And then I said, “Why not indicate that it’s my last Sunday at Westminster?” Later in the week, the organist phoned in the music for Sunday, including the titles for prelude, offertory and postlude. When the calendar appeared, this is how it read:           Sunday. June 24, 1979           Dr. Vipond’s last Sunday at Westminster           Organ Prelude: “Now Thank We All Our God” Apparently, someone from Britain was visiting Westminster that day and showed PUNCH the calendar! Is there such a thing as a Freudian slip? The case rests!
