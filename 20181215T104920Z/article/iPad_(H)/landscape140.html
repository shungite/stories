The latter club commenced in 1947 holding regular monthly meetings with an average of 20 couples enjoying fellowship and related activities.The highlight of this section of the 50-50 Club was the presentation of a Marriage Clinic Series in 1949, the first of three clinics under a committee made up of Rhiney Friesen, Betty Morrison, Alex McNair, Bonnie Kerr, Rev. Reid Vipond ,Associate Minister, and Rev. Allen Huband, Minister. Four lectures were presented — Dr. Stephen (?) from Saskatoon on the Physiology of Marriage- (attendance-350), Lawyer H. G. Harvey Smith on Legal Aspects of Marriage- (attendance-400), Dr. Carl Henneberg and Dr. Jessie McGeach on the Physical Side of Marriage- (attendance- 600- 400 men, 200 women), Rev,. Allen Huband on the Spiritual Aspect of marriage (attendance-300). These lectures were so successful that the series was put on again in 1950 and 1951 with excellent results. Outside of regular programming on a monthly basis the Club organized lectures on: Music presented by our bass soloist, Ed Forrest, and organist and choir master Herb Sadler. Interior Decorating was presented by Prof. John Russell, and Landscape Gardening by Hector McDonald, to name a few, of these presentations. There were Christmas dances, film nights with Harry Easton and Jack Pierce, summer picnics at which, again, children from the Protestant Orphans’ Home were our guests. Weiner roasts and other activities filled out the year. Another successful venture was the Club’s Travelling Road Show which put on a presentation at fowl suppers in places like Warren, Stonewall, Argyle, Teulon, and Balmoral Manitoba over a period of time. Soloists such as Doug Sommerville, and Paul Fredette were accompanied by Bonnie Kerr plus a choir made up of club members. To compliment these programs, members Bruce Rutherford, Alex McNair, Charlie Fred and Murray Kerr provided comedy skits. Like the earlier edition of the Club, people’s interests changed, some moved on to other areas, and as a result the 50-50 Club slowly dissolved. **In 1952 The College for Home Knowledge was organized to take its place but interest waned and this project was given up. In 1954 the Christian Education Committee established The Mr. and Mrs. Club for younger married couples.
