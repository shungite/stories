Concerning choir activities at that time; The Men’s Musical Club of Winnipeg had started The Manitoba Musical Festival in May, 1919. Westminster Choir entered the competition for their first time in the Festival’s first year. Then after an absence in the competition of 10 years we were again performing in 1930 when we won the Grade B Shield for church choirs. Then we won the Grade A Shield in 1933, 1935, and 1936. During the ministry of our fourth minister, Dr. John Sutherland Bonnell, Herb reports, “we were forced to hold two Easter Morning Services — identical in every respect- to accommodate the crowds who came to worship.” This practice of dual services at Easter continued into the ministry of our eighth minister, Rev. Reid Vipond. Reid felt so sorry for the choir’s having to do double service Easter morning he not only provided us with the coffee and hot cross buns between the services, he also preached a different sermon at each service so we wouldn’t be bored! As the numbers in the congregation declined (from 1948 onward) the Easter two-service practice was eventually dropped during Rev. Bob Thompson’s time. Evening services, of course, had been stopped before that during the ministry of Rev. Nelson Mercer (our seventh minister). For the choir, life became so much simpler as the years went on! Herb’s account of the choir also refers to concerts, performed at churches in rural Manitoba. Radio, too, was a fact of choir life in the 1930s. He notes us broadcasting, “for the Canadian Radio Commission such major works as Mozart’s Requiem  and Dvořák’s Stabat Mater .” In 1936 and 1937 the choir represented Winnipeg in the Commission’s National Christmas Day Cross-Country Hook-up.
