Ernest Marshall Howse Part Two: His work at Westminster In a nutshell: He returned to Canada in 1935 to become the minister at Westminster United Church which was located in central Winnipeg. Westminster served some of the poorest areas of Canada during the Great Depression. Howse became a prominent advocate for the social gospel movement. He also believed in a very liberal view of Christianity. For example, he did not believe in the physical resurrection of Jesus. In 1948 he moved to Toronto to become minister at Bloor Street United Church, a position he would retain until his retirement in 1970. The Howse family (Ernest, Esther and daughter Margaret) arrived at the beginning of winter, a rude change from the climate in California. Alternatively, when summer arrived it was the hottest on record for Winnipeg. (The author of this piece was a small child at that time and remembers our sleeping in the basement of our house that summer to escape some of that suffocating heat at night.
