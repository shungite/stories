Rev. Robert Campbell In our 125 year journey as a congregation Robert Campbell was our tenth minister. He came to us in 1989 and left in 2015. At 26 years this is the longest term of service in Westminster besting the previous record holder, Rev. David Christie, our third minister, whose term of service spanned twenty years. In rough mathematical calculation Robert's ministry occupied twenty percent of Westminster's whole history. Robert, in every respect a native Manitoban, was born in Hamiota in 1953. For those whose Manitoba geography is fuzzy, Hamiota is located in the Westman region of the province (think Brandon, Carberry Desert, Minnedosa, etc.) His first five years of life was on his parents’ farm located near Isabella (south of Riding Mountain National Park). Then in 1959 the family moved to Roblin, (northwest of Riding Mountain) where he completed his schooling. As a matter of interest Robert is one of five in the family, his sister Doris and brothers Malcolm, Neil and Langdon being the other four. In 1969 he was confirmed at Knox United Church in Roblin  and would later be named a candidate for ministry from Knox. From 1971-75 he studied for his Arts degree at Brandon University. Then in 1975 he enrolled at Emmanuel College in Toronto to study for the ministry. In 1978 he was ordained for ministry at the Manitoba / Northwestern Ontario Conference Annual Meeting, held that year in Ashern  in the Inter lake area of the province. 1978 was also the year he and Linda Cohoe were married. They settled in Holland where Robert’s ministry was in the Holland / Cypress River area. Then, in 1989, they moved to Winnipeg where Robert took up his duties at Westminster. In 1993, James Fraser Douglas Campbell appeared on the scene. The writer remembers well the night Linda appeared at Church Choir practice, face beaming, with baby James in her arms. The family was complete.
