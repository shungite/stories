So the stone was laid and the steps reappeared. Towards the end of the stone masonry part of the project the workers had a little fun and set up their own inuksuks, Inuit symbols from Canada’s frozen North. Actual construction started April 2018. The stone part is complete. All that is missing is the handrail that helps people up the centre of the structure. As we are a Heritage building and subject to certain controls we can’t use the steps till that railing is installed.           For all this we have Brandon Johnston to thank in three directions: he designed the project and identified the construction company,
