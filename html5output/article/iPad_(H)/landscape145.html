Reid felt so sorry for the choir’s having to do double service Easter morning he not only provided us with the coffee and hot cross buns between the services, he also preached a different sermon at each service so we wouldn’t be bored! As the numbers in the congregation declined (from 1948 onward) the Easter two-service practice was eventually dropped during Rev. Bob Thompson’s time. Evening services, of course, had been stopped before that during the ministry of Rev. Nelson Mercer (our seventh minister). For the choir, life became so much simpler as the years went on! Herb’s account of the choir also refers to concerts, performed at churches in rural Manitoba. Radio, too, was a fact of choir life in the 1930s. He notes us broadcasting, “for the Canadian Radio Commission such major works as Mozart’s Requiem  and Dvořák’s Stabat Mater .” In 1936 and 1937 the choir represented Winnipeg in the Commission’s National Christmas Day Cross-Country Hook-up. Notes on Our Soloists and Other Friends A partial lis of soloists during Sadler’s incumbency in its early years would include Herber Hutton in the 1920s, and, later, Mrs. D.Sims Hare, Mrs. Dr. Sharman, Roy Wideman , Fitz Herbert Hughes. In the 1930s were George Kent and Helen Tennent — who joined the choir then and stayed till the late 40s when Jean Ramsay replaced her. Ruth Matheson, alto, also joined in the in the fall of 1931 but didn’t last as long as Helen. Ruth was succeeded by Margaret Norrie, aunt of Bill Norrie-later our Mayor — and she in turn by Myfanwy Evans. Ed Forrest was the bass soloist in the 40’s and was succeeded by Paul Fredette late 1951. An interesting note on these people of the quartet, in the Music Festival our quartet won the Shield for church quartets in 1931, 1933, and 1934. In 1951, On Palm Sunday evening-March 18, Ed Forrest presented the new lyrics of the “negro spiritual,” Were You There , written by minister, Rev. Allan Huband.
