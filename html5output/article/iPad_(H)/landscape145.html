For an article on the Premier Performance of Messiah (April 13, 1742) go to https://www.goethe.de/ins/ie/en/kul/sup/dsi/20733404.html By 1956 Norma Lewiki replaced Jean Ramsey as soprano soloist, and Nina Vanderlinden was our alto. Nina had started her time at Westminster as Nina Biniowski. But the handsome young man in the bass section, Graham Vanderlinden, romanced her to marriage. This met with general Doug Sommerville approval. Also, in 1956 Ted Alford was tenor soloist. He had replaced George Robson who had held that position since the late 1940s. Doug Sommerville was our baritone soloist having replaced Paul Fredette who had left Winnipeg. We looked forward to Doug’s solos. They always told a story. George Robson Barry Anderson Our organist from the time of Herb’s death (mid 1955) was one of his students, Allen Borbridge, who was both organist and choirmaster for two years. Barry Anderson then took on the job of organist and Glen Harrison became our Choir Director on September 15, 1957. Barry was a student of both Herb Sadler and Hugh Bancroft. He was not only a fine organist, he had considerable skill as a sight reader and an awesome ability to transpose music on the spot. He was with us till 1966 when he moved to Knox Church as their organist.
