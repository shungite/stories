Stories of Westminster United Church & its People / Page 165
Nancy Finley’s memories: “My first memory of our Unit is of the phone ringing in my kitchen and when I answered it was Mary Leithead from Westminster United Church (WUC). She was asking me to come out to a Unit meeting the next Wednesday at her house. My question to all and sundry is: how do you say no to Mary Leithead? I wanted to, that’s for sure. A Unit (or Circle) was something my Mother had belonged to and was meant for her age group. I told Mary I had Meaghan (3 months old at the time) and really couldn’t see my way to join any group. Dave and I had been married at WUC and maybe attended a few Sunday services, but that was all. Mary being Mary laughed and told me to bring Meaghan (said lots did) and that she would ask a lovely lady by the name of Joan Brathwaite (now Joan Wilton) to pick me up. It was settled, I was going. Joan picked me up and WAS a lovely lady. I remember thinking that if the rest of the group was this nice, great, the evening would be fine. The evening was fun, chatting with women in the same boat as me. Trying to live a Christian life while raising kids and dealing with life’s adventures. It wasn’t that long before we (as a group) decided to look after the nursery Sunday school, thusly meeting more of our Church members and becoming involved. I have a clear memory of making ‘fancy’ sandwiches at Lorrie’s house for the Annual Fall Bazaar (maybe it was the spring tea) and that skill has served me well through the years. As a group we were definitely each other’s significant others when the need arose. This has proven true to this day. We are so lucky. But mostly I remember having fun. Dinners with the Husbands (once again at Lorrie’s house — she had the largest home at the time or was the first to volunteer to host). I truly believe that because of Unit I, became more involved with WUC. We all did. We welcomed new members to our group and said goodbye to others. We chose studies that were relevant to our lives and shared thoughts and feelings this way.”
