Stories of Westminster United Church & its People / Page 86
Theodore Roosevelt Jr.was married there, Wendel Wilke as previously noted was commemorated there, Duke Ellington performed there, the mayor of New York worshipped there. However, beyond celebrity the congregation, started in 1808 was widely respected for it’s outreach work. In 1815, they established New York’s first free schools which later became the city’s public school system. They have had a vigorous program of relief work among the poor and homeless in the city. And they boast, on their website of having started Dial-a-Prayer. Enter John Sutherland Bonnell, stage right! His sermons at Fifth Avenue were regularly reported each week in the New York Times . The topics he preached on were similar to those heard by Westminster’s congregation including his fears of communist Russia and its negative effect on religious worship. For example excerpts from his Fifth Avenue sermon, Has Russia Banished God. “History has witnessed many attempts to abolish religion and banish God. My text for this morning refers to one of these. Antiochus Epiphanes, who became the King of Syria in the year 175 B.C. undertook the subjugation of Palestine. It was his policy to conquer and Hellenized as much of the world as possible. The Greek King made a surprise attack upon Jerusalem and captured the city. After having massacred many of its inhabitants, he established his military forces within the temple area and declared that Jewish religious observances were illegal. All sacrifice was abolished. The sacred books were destroyed; and even Sabbath observance was prohibited. But the crowning indignity of all was committed by Antiochus, when, in December168 B.C., he set up an altar to a pagan deity in the heart of the temple and then, to complete the desecration in the eyes of the Jews, he sacrificed swine upon that altar. This was the abomination that maketh desolate. So great was the indignation of the Hebrew people that the Maccabees, the father and his five sons, led a spontaneous uprising against the King, who, when he saw his purpose frustrated, went mad, and died shortly thereafter.”
Rev. John Sutherland Bonnell
