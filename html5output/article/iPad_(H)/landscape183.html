From those positions pressure was exerted to introduce the concept of Board at Westminster, which was realized in 1970 with Marilyn elected as the first Chair. A side note: In 1970 I , (R.H.),  was responsible for organizing the service of Communion  four times a year ( a task I had for ten years.). My job was to find 10 or 12 volunteers to be the servers each Communion.. By tradition the servers were always men. Shortly after the new Board took office Marilyn Huband phoned me: M: Roy, you are responsible for recruiting communion servers are you not? R: Yes, I am. M: At the next Communion will you please ensure that women and young people are involved      as servers, not just men? R: I'll do my best. M: Roy, it is absolutely essential. Women and young people must be included from now on.
