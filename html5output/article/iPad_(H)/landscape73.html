Stories of Westminster United Church & its People / Page 70
In the ‘40s the woman of the church produced incredible numbers of afghans, quilts, layettes and other knitted articles during WWII. The Sewing Circle distributed items to the Christmas Cheer Board and other mission-and-help organizations in the city. Up to 1970 we organized a Women’s Missionary Society and a Community Services Committee. Our people were involved in organizing pre-natal programs, a Sunshine Club for seniors and a hot-lunch program for the elderly in the community. In 1965 we created a Child Care program which eventually became the Day care program operated out of Westminster today. Since 1970 our Outreach Committee has organized donations for International Hope, supported the rehabilitation housing project on Maryland Street, supported West Broadway Ministry, Art City, United For Peace Project, The Council of Women, Ma Mawi and Agape Table, and provided clothing donations for Westminster Day Care, and Mulvey School. Of course there has had to be a large number of people in the congregation to accomplish much of all this activity. Many of the people involved were not employed outside their homes, especially the women who worked unstintingly to create mountains of material for people all over the world whom they never knew and would never meet. It was a personal thing. You don’t have money. But you have yourself, your skills. Reaching out was a way of life in our community of caring people.
