A large Bible was kept on the pulpit which visitors and preachers signed. Names such as Dr. Grenfell   of Labrador, Ramsey MacDonald, former Prime Minister of Britain, Rev. Professor Moffat (translator of the Bible) and many others signed. Sad to relate vandal stole that historic Bible and it has never been recovered. Times and customs of dress change. On a hot Sunday in June, 1923, I was inducted as an elder.It was usual to wear a dark suit, tie, etc. and white shirt. My suited been to the cleaners and it reeked of gasoline. The new elder next to me kept sniffing during the ceremony. I was warm enough on that hot Sunday, but became more uncomfortable when the minister announced the title of his sermon; he kept repeating it and seemed to look my way each time. The title of the sermon was ‘Christian Fragrance.’ One event each year was the congregational picnic at the parks. One year we hired a C.N.R. four or five coach train, sold tickets all over Winnipeg and spent the Saturday afternoon at Victoria Beach. They gave me $200.00 to buy prizes and we spent it all. When the races started every person or stranger at Victoria Beach turned up to get in on the races and the grand prize list. Senator John T. Haig acted as our policeman and saw to it that no-one took part unless they were connected with Westminster. In the early twenties many of the large homes in the vicinity of Westminster were occupied by our members and their large families. The result was our Sunday School had a large attendance and at one time we had the largest Sunday School enrolment of any Presbyterian Church in Canada. We had a boys’ department of around 150 boys between 12 and 17. This Department had been well organized by the late Walter MacDonald and he had a powerful executive which met frequently on plans and projects — Colonel J.D. Sinclair, T.D. Patton, M. McCracken, B.V. Richardson, Wm. Bonellie, J. R. Major, and I was included as I was running the gym classes twice a week.  Class space was hard to find. Two classes in the kitchen, 6 or 7 on the gym floor (Lower Hall), one each in the hall, the men's washroom, the vestry, and the  shower dressing room ( the latter usually erupted  in weekly disaster).
