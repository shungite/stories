I remember thinking that if the rest of the group was this nice, great, the evening would be fine. The evening was fun, chatting with women in the same boat as me. Trying to live a Christian life while raising kids and dealing with life’s adventures. It wasn’t that long before we (as a group) decided to look after the nursery Sunday school, thusly meeting more of our Church members and becoming involved. I have a clear memory of making ‘fancy’ sandwiches at Lorrie’s house for the Annual Fall Bazaar (maybe it was the spring tea) and that skill has served me well through the years. As a group we were definitely each other’s significant others when the need arose. This has proven true to this day. We are so lucky. But mostly I remember having fun. Dinners with the Husbands (once again at Lorrie’s house — she had the largest home at the time or was the first to volunteer to host). I truly believe that because of Unit I, became more involved with WUC. We all did. We welcomed new members to our group and said goodbye to others. We chose studies that were relevant to our lives and shared thoughts and feelings this way.” Unit 10, too, is still going strong, celebrating their 50 year mark as a unit within the church, Phyllis Reader reports. They are going to the Temple Gardens Spa in Moose Jaw to celebrate this auspicious occasion at the end of the month! Unit 10 meets once a month during the winter months and has a special dinner at Christmas and spring. Liz Witjkamp, Rita Menzies, Val McIntyre, Lynn Heise and Ann Peeler have been members since the beginning. Phyllis goes on to say the group “was a great support, as members went through the various stages of life, career changes, raising kids, grandchildren, weddings, funerals and parents passing away. We did our share of teas and helping with after church luncheons, garage sales, and contributions of home baking for a host of events. We've been through it all and have developed very strong, loyal relationships over the years. We met in members homes once a month. Discussed church business and all of us have served on the various committees and the church board. In fact they are now recycling us again.”
