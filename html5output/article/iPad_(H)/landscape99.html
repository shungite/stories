Stories of Westminster United Church & its People / Page 97
Education in the early days of NFLD was very basic. The local school was (and still is) owned and operated by the church, whatever denomination of “Church” was to be found in each outpost be it Roman Catholic, Anglican, Methodist, or later, Salvation Army Outpost settlements were normally all people- one denomination. To be sure that the local school reflected the values of the local church the minister of the community’s church was Chair of the Board. Ernest remarks how, at age 18, working in his first pastoral charge as a probationer ( a student minister in charge of a community church) in the remote village of Burgeo, he became the instant Chairman of the School Board. His mother gave him her most precious possession, an Unabridged Webster Dictionary to help in this aspect of his work. Education in outpost schools was basic and the quality of teaching varied from outpost to outpost. He mentions, with considerable pride that despite the”leanness” of the basic education system five of the Howse family of six children earned University degrees and the sixth became an R.N. an incredible accomplishment. One of his siblings became a missionary in Angola, another a Government Geologist, another a Marine Biologist, himself an ordained minister. As mentioned, Ernest’s father, Charles, was originally a fisherman; at age 19 he was a skipper of his own boat and by faith an Anglican but at age 22 his life changed completely. Attending a Methodist Revival, Charles made a personal decision to become a Methodist. He left fishing and, with his brothers’ support, went to St. John’s to the Methodist College ( what we would call high school) and received his A.A. Diploma, Associate of Arts. This diploma would be accepted by Canadian universities as qualified entrance education. Having no money to attend university Charles worked for several years as a school teacher. When he had raised the requisite money he left NFLD, and attended Mt. Allison University in N.B, and then, on to Garret Biblical Institute in Chicago. After graduation he returned to NFLD where he lived out his life as a minister.
Rev. Ernest Marshall Howse
