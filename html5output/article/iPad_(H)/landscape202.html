Doug, who had enrolled in Grade 9 at River Heights Junior High when we came to Winnipeg in 1963, went on to Kelvin High and then to do his BA at the University of Winnipeg, graduating with the gold medal for highest standing in the general arts course. He had worked each summer (on the end of a hoe) at the University of Manitoba farm, and had a bank account to go to York University in Toronto in the fall of 1970 to pursue a PhD in psychology. So, in our huge manse at 141 Waterloo Street (3,200 square feet of floor space and a 100 × 150 foot property), we were down to one family member at home. Rob had proceeded through elementary, junior high, and was now (1970–1971) in his final year at Kelvin High School and had made his decision to go east for his university work. We certainly didn’t need this huge manse. Dorothy was already on part-time staff at Laura Second as teacher-librarian, having taken the necessary five courses at U of M. A smaller home would suit us better. It would also give us opportunity to get some equity in a house, so that upon retirement we wouldn’t simply leave the keys on the kitchen counter and face the prospect of having to buy a home at that late stage. I spoke to some of the people on the board who had been around for years about buying our own house, although I assured them that if the congregation felt strongly about retaining its manse we could no doubt accommodate ourselves to their decision. Fortunately, the church board was very happy to get out of the property business. The manse was sold. We purchased a very suitable three- bedroom bungalow at 718 Waterloo Street, and one of Rob’s last jobs at home before going to U of T was to help us with the back-breaking job of moving our goods and chattels just five blocks down the street. We hired movers, of course, to move the piano, living room and bedroom furniture and appliances, but did the rest ourselves. And we did it all in one day, June 30, 1971. The people from whom we bought didn’t move out till June 29, and the people who bought 141 wanted to move in on July 1. With a rented two-wheel trailer, and the help of some of Rob’s friends with a station wagon, we made an untold number of trips up and down Waterloo Street.
