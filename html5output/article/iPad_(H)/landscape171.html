In 1925, at the time of Union, it is noted about one quarter of the missionary work of the United Church was supported by the women of the Women’s Missionary Society The records show that in 1928, the Women’s Auxiliary of Westminster had a membership of 78, and contributed $1,800 to the work of The Women’s Missionary Society. There were 32 in the Mission Band that year, that contributed $200.00. I wanted to share this motto of the Women’s Missionary Society from those early years: “The World for Christ Not by might nor by power, but by my spirit, saith the Lord of Hosts. Whatsoever He saith unto you, do it.” It was the ‘Do It’ that spoke to me, and continues to speak to Westminster women. I’d like to share with you a bit about what the work of the Women’s Auxiliary supported. This is from a 1928 report by a Dr. Caroline Macdonald, who did “Prison Work” — these early women who did home missions were called deaconesses, the early beginnings of the diaconal ministry we have today. Dr. Macdonald states: “Statistics may be very uninteresting, but can sometimes be very instructive. You will remember that the grant I am receiving from the United Church of Canada is $2,500 a year. I have just finished making out the half yearly statement of how the money has been used and I was curious myself to see what information statistics alone would bring forth. Here they are: with about $620. We have sent 1,406 books to 326 men in prison. With less than $700, we have given the equivalent of one night’s lodging to about 1,474 men or an average of 4 men for an entire year, as well as other financial help to 202 others. The number of letters received from men in prison was 679 and number sent 907, while 1,420 ex prisoners have been given advice about everything under the sun, including things spiritual and mundane. We have sent men home to the country when they have wanted to go, we have bought clothes for them when they were needed, we have set them up in business. We have mended family quarrels, we have brought wives as well as men back to a sense of duty, we have stayed awake nights thinking of those who have gone astray again, and we have stopped at nothing if by all means we might save some.” For Caroline MacDonald’s bio check out https://www.renewal-fellowship.ca/93
