James, the Christie’s sixth child was born in Edinburgh. “… a man of affairs and a preacher with power.” The congregation of St. Matthews United Free Presbyterian Church in Glasgow knew that David Christie was the man they wanted. They sent him a call in late October, 1903. The Christies accepted this call and moved their six children to Glasgow. The Winnipeg Tribune  noted, when the Christies later received their call to Westminster, that St. Matthews “is considered one of the most important charges in Scotland and Mr. Christie proved no unworthy successor to the eminent divine whom he followed. By his genial personality, outstanding ability and interest in social problems he gained a reputation second to none among the younger men of the United Free church.” David had arrived, so it seemed, in his profession. He was to stay in Glasgow until 1909. The family grew with the births of son, David, and son, Archibald. Life was good to and for the Christies at this time. In September, ’09, the Dundee Evening Telegraph  reported: A Canadian cablegram states that a cordial and enthusiastic call has been extended by the congregation of Westminster Presbyterian Church, Winnipeg to the Rev. David Christie … the call, which is characterized as the most unanimous and hearty ever issued in Western Canada has been rendered necessary owing to the appointment of the Rev. Dr. McKinnon to the principalship of Halifax College (that is, Pine Hills Theological College). When this call to the far away centre of North America arrived, in David’s words, “like a bomb in the Manse,” one might imagine Margaret and the eight youngsters — Move? Where?! Winni-what? The call was accepted. The family packed their worldly goods in steamer trunks. On October 30th they left Glasgow on the three year old steamer, Hesparian , for an eight day journey across the North Atlantic (at a speed of 15 knots), up the St. Lawrence River to Quebec; off the boat and onto the C.P.R. train bound for Winnipeg. They arrived on November 9th, met at the train station by a large crowd. Then off to their new home at 49 Furby Street. David preached his first Westminster sermons (morning and evening services) Sunday, November 14th.
