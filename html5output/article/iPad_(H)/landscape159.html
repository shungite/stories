Amanda Tiwari. We met upstairs every Sunday 9:30 to 10:00 from Sept to May. The Membership ranged from six to twelve singers over the next fifteen years. We sang once a month during the worship service and always at the early Christmas Eve service as well as many musicals over the years. We also were invited to sing at services at Riverview, Lions Place, and Poseidon Centre and a Christmas program at the Festival of Trees in 2000. One thing remained constant — donut eggs as a treat at the end of practice! There was an old upright piano at the beginning which was replaced by a lovely piano received from the estate of Donald Smith in 2005. It was always lovely to hear the young voices gain confidence and strength during the year. At least two of the members, Robert McMahon and James Campbell went on to study voice as adults. Glen Harrison, the Senior Choir and the congregation were always so supportive and encouraging. On two occasions we were invited to sing a combined anthem with the Senior Choir which was a huge thrill for the kids. These are the musicals presented by the choir and Sunday School students: 1997 and 2002 “The Rich Man, the Builder and The Feast” 1998 and 2004 “What Child Was This?” 2000 “No Doubt About It” 2005 “Amahl and the Night Visitor” with the Senior Choir 2006 “The Lion and the Lamb” 2008 “The Promise” The 2004 production of What Child Is This?
