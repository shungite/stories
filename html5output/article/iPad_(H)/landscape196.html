At this point, we changed direction quite considerably in our staffing, and decided to hire three staff people working one-third time each. Joan McConnell, who had been a teacher before she and Barry had four children, was our Sunday school superintendent, and she became our director of Christian education. Audrey McLennan, who, before she and Norm had their three boys, had been a program director at the YWCA, was anxious to get back into part-time work and was happy to work one-third time as outreach coordinator. Rev. Bill Conly, just retired after serving all his forty years in Manitoba, became our one-third-time retired visiting assistant. The four of us formed a very happy working relationship, each with her or his special responsibilities. Bill Conly’s wife, Irene, was one of the Westminster Riddell family, so for them it was like coming home. Bill was with us until my retirement in 1979. Joan McConnell went with Barry to Britain for a year’s sabbatical in 1978, and Elaine Hickerson, wife of Rev. Bill Hickerson, secretary of Manitoba conference, came as her replacement. Audrey McLennan also left in 1978 to work full- time at her home church, Fort Garry, and Orma Backhouse replaced her. Partly as a footnote, and as a point of interest, Joan McConnell is now personnel secretary of Manitoba conference. Audrey McLennan later became president of Manitoba conference, and now chairs the department at head office that produces Spirit Connection  on Vision TV. The music department saw fewer changes than any other part of the staff. Herb Sadler was organist and choir director from 1919–1953. Glen Harrison then assumed the position of choir director—and is still there! Barry Anderson was organist for several years, and was succeeded in 1967 by Don Menzies, who is also still in that position. With a fine, four-manual Casavant organ, and excellent organists and choir directors, Westminster has had superlative music throughout its long history. In our years, Glen Harrison directed many special choir recitals, and on numerous occasions had a half dozen Winnipeg Symphony players for orchestral accompaniment. Don Menzies, as well as guest organists, have played (and broadcast) many recitals. I have heard both Marcel Dupré and E. Power Biggs in recital on that outstanding instrument.
