And finally we let John Sutherland Bonnell speak for himself. Courage to See It Through Excerpted from a sermon by the Rev. John Sutherland Bonnell, DD, LLD, Minister of The Fifth Avenue Presbyterian Church, New York City, February 25, 1945. While the Pharisees were gathered together, Jesus asked them, saying, What think ye of Christ? — St. Matthew 22:41-42 At the beginning of this century there arose a school of writers who tried to explain away our Lord by suggesting that He never really lived. He was an ‘ideal’ and possessed no objective existence. He was created in the imagination of His followers. But, these writers failed to realize that they had simply pushed the problem one step backward. Once you declare that Jesus is a myth, you must produce a man who possessed the loftiest spiritual insights of the ages; who spoke the beatitudes; who wrote the parables of the Good Samaritan and the Prodigal Son; who uttered the imperishable words of the Sermon on the Mount; who created the most radiant, the most tender, the most glorious personality of all time. And having found a man capable of this colossal achievement you would have found one worthy of the veneration of mankind. As Joseph Parker has said: “Only a Christ could have conceived a Christ!” In other words, the inventor would have been as great as his hero. Here is one issue that is settled forever. The pages of the New Testament carry the “authentic marks” of a mighty creative personality. We have no biography of Jesus it is true. But we have the records and the impressions of the men who walked and talked with Him and from the Gospels there emerges one consistent character whose face we can see and to whose words we may listen.
Rev. John Sutherland Bonnell
