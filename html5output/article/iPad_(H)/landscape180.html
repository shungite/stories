You will remember that the grant I am receiving from the United Church of Canada is $2,500 a year. I have just finished making out the half yearly statement of how the money has been used and I was curious myself to see what information statistics alone would bring forth. Here they are: with about $620. We have sent 1,406 books to 326 men in prison. With less than $700, we have given the equivalent of one night’s lodging to about 1,474 men or an average of 4 men for an entire year, as well as other financial help to 202 others. The number of letters received from men in prison was 679 and number sent 907, while 1,420 ex prisoners have been given advice about everything under the sun, including things spiritual and mundane. We have sent men home to the country when they have wanted to go, we have bought clothes for them when they were needed, we have set them up in business. We have mended family quarrels, we have brought wives as well as men back to a sense of duty, we have stayed awake nights thinking of those who have gone astray again, and we have stopped at nothing if by all means we might save some.”

Westminster had a very direct connection with a medical missionary, Margaret Coltart, R.N. She was actually a member of Westminster who served in India. Here is a bit about her and her work in Central India. “Many splendid women medical doctors and nurses have made a contribution as labourers in this part of Christ’s vineyard, including Dr. Laura Moodie, Dr. Mina McKenzie, Dr. Maud Smith, Dr. Catherine Whittier, and Dr. Lakshman. Miss Margaret Coltart, RN, now in charge, came first to Hat Piplia, India, in 1919, serving till 1924, then from 1930 to 1934 and returning in 1938.
