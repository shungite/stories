Glenys Hughes, also from Covenant College, succeeded Susan, and less than two years later married Rev. Harry Oussoren and moved back to Toronto. Barbara Bryant also came to us when she completed her two-year course at Covenant College, but stayed for only one year. Upon leaving Westminster, Barbara attended Seneca       College in Toronto and graduated in early childhood education. She married a minister and they serve jointly on a pastoral charge in Saskatchewan. When Barbara was leaving, and Jack Mathieson retired, the board decided we should move into a new staffing arrangement and they called a relatively young ordained minister, Cecil Tiller. He seemed anxious to move west from Saint John, New Brunswick, and to undertake a role similar to the one that I had earlier done at Westminster, i.e., to be an associate minister with major responsibility for Christian education, to do as much visiting as possible, and to preach once each month. This arrangement seemed to work well for a year or two, but Cec began to realize that he really preferred preaching to CE work, and accepted a call to St. Andrew’s Church in Regina after two years at Westminster. At this point, we changed direction quite considerably in our staffing, and decided to hire three staff people working one-third time each. Joan McConnell, who had been a teacher before she and Barry had four children, was our Sunday school superintendent, and she became our director of Christian education. Audrey McLennan, who, before she and Norm had their three boys, had been a program director at the YWCA, was anxious to get back into part-time work and was happy to work one-third time as outreach coordinator. Rev. Bill Conly, just retired after serving all his forty years in Manitoba, became our one-third-time retired visiting assistant. The four of us formed a very happy working relationship, each with her or his special responsibilities. Bill Conly’s wife, Irene, was one of the Westminster Riddell family, so for them it was like coming home. Bill was with us until my retirement in 1979. Joan McConnell went with Barry to Britain for a year’s sabbatical in 1978, and Elaine Hickerson, wife of Rev. Bill Hickerson, secretary of Manitoba conference, came as her replacement.
