Pat Hodges arrived in Winnipeg with her husband, Houston, just as the search committee was looking for a new church secretary. Houston was a United  Methodist minister from Texas, and had come to be the social action secretary of Winnipeg presbytery. Joan McConnell met the Hodges, discovered that Pat had been a church secretary in the deep south and was hoping to get employment in Winnipeg. It was a perfect fit, and for our last two years at Westminster, the phone was answered with a happy and cheery southern drawl. For many years, indeed, from its beginning in 1892, Westminster had a Session and a Deacon’s Court. The Deacon’s Court was something like an official board in that it consisted of all members of Session, plus people who were on the finance and property committee. There was no Committee of Stewards; this function was performed by the Deacon’s Court. Somewhere in Dr. Allen Huband’s tenure (1948–1955), the decision was made to follow the United Church Manual which called for a Session, a Committee of Stewards, and an Official Board. In general terms, the Session has oversight over things spiritual, the Stewards over things temporal, and the Official Board is a combination of these two (plus presidents of all organizations). Also, it has the power to act on behalf of the congregation. But there has always been a problem here. Why should we divide the spiritual and the temporal? Aren’t they all of a piece, really? Why should one particular group (the Stewards) do all the property work, and even more especially, all the financial decisions? Granted, all decisions are supposed to be checked by the Official Board, but this is usually only rubber-stamping. Also, it makes difficult communication within a church. Despite the Good Book to the contrary, the left hand should know what the right is doing! Another problem with the board structure at Westminster (common in many churches) was that there seemed to be no terminal point for many office holders. For example, when we went to Westminster in 1963, the same man had been clerk of Session for a dozen years or more and the same person had held the position as chairman of Stewards for ten years.
