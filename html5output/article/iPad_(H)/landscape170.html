Unit 10-Ann Peeler, Phyllis Reader, Val McIntyre, Elizabeth Wijtkamp, Lynn Heise, Marnie Bolland United Church Women (from the Internet) When the United Church officially formed in 1925, the Ladies Aid (LA) (later the Woman’s Association (WA)) and the Women’s Missionary Society (WMS) were doing work at home and abroad. The WA focused on supporting the local church and community, while the WMS was more involved with education and world mission. When Westminster congregation was created in 1892 some of the first groups (or clubs) to form were Missional in focus, a Women’s Missionary group, a Young Women's Missionary group, and later a Pitblado Missionary group. The major task of Church outreach was in the hands of its women. At the same time women of our church continued their traditional roles as teachers, fundraisers, and organizers of social functions, teas and dinners.
