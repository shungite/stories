Our text tells us that: While the Pharisees were gathered together, Jesus asked them, saying, What think ye of Christ? “What do you think of Christ?” Your answer may not shed much light on the personality  of our Lord, but it will be an intimate revelation of yourself. What do you think of Christ? What is your opinion of Him? What do you make of his life and teaching? What do you think of this Great Exemplar who revealed human life as God intends it to be lived? To follow Christ today calls for all and more than all the courage we possess. It demands a quality of moral heroism. Even the basest of men respect loyalty that cannot be purchased. Let me say now to every one of you young men and women in the uniform of your country: if yon rise up and follow Christ it will demand every atom of moral courage you possess to see it through. Listen to the words of an old Christian warrior: "Endure hardness as a good soldier of Jesus Christ." This is not a call to ease and safety. It's a challenge to hardship, to battle, to wounds, but it carries the promise of final victory. Here is a young Man walking along a highway of Galilee with His friends. For almost three years they have journeyed together. He stands at a cross-road, lost in thought. He is young, only thirty- three years of age. He is in love with life. He wants to live, to carry on His work, to enjoy His friends. He yearns. In every fiber of His being for the life he is ready to relinquish. He stands at the cross-road and looks to the North. There the pleasant fields and flowers and friends await Him, people who truly loved Him, and whom He dearly loved. If He takes the road to the South-to Jerusalem, He will meet with hostility, opposition, plottings, betrayal, arrest, trial, and crucifixion-the cruelest of all deaths. He stands there, straight and tall, battling it out to a decision. Could we blame Him if He hesitates? But now the die is cast. His face is set steadfastly to the South. Jesus of His own free will chose the road to the cross. That is sheer courage, courage which will never be surpassed …
Rev. John Sutherland Bonnell
