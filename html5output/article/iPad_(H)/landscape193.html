The Outreach Committee and the Board of the Church accepted this application and it went through the process with Immigration Canada. Due to the 9/11 attacks in New York City in 2001 there were numerous delays. On Sept. 29th, 2003 Khalil Raza arrived at the Winnipeg airport to the absolute joy of his brother, Allahyar. The church gave him $500.00 for clothing and some miscellaneous items to get started. He quickly became employed and excelled in his new home. He is married and now lives in Vancouver, B.C. Again a very successful sponsorship. 3) Eric Rivera and his wife, Indrani Ospina and 4 sons had fled Columbia, South America in 1997. Their journey to Canada was filled with terror and uncertainty. They were living in Winnipeg. On April 22, 2002 they came to Westminster United Church asking for financial help. Their application with Immigration Canada was due to run out on June 30th, 2002 and it cost $1300.00 for the application. With the support of the Outreach Committee the money was given to them from the Refugee Fund. They have remained in Winnipeg and went on to be excellent Canadian citizens. The Refugee Committee was recreated again in April, 2014. Phyllis Reader was the chair and Val McIntyre was her assistant. (Val at left)They created an application form for “family reunification” sponsorship with Westminster United Church. The rationale was the church would not be financially responsible for the refugee but the requesting applicant would take full responsibility for their family member. The Betty Halstead Refugee Fund would act as a slush fund if needed. There were several applicants. Phyllis and Val did interviews with recorded notes. In all cases the applicants proved not to be appropriate for the mandate of the committee. The committee is on hold at the present time due to the financial and ministerial circumstances of the church. This report is written for the sole purpose of information and archival material on Refugee Sponsorships at Westminster United Church over the years.
