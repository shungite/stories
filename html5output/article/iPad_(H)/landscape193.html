Community Clubs and schools did little; in fact few Community Clubs existed. Sunday School leagues had a large organization take care of athletic meets, swim meets, basketball, hockey, soccer, lacrosse for the entire city. Westminster generally did very well and some photos are in our church vestibule.  Winning medals were provided in all leagues by the Griffon Club of Winnipeg to all sections from Midget to Senior. We had a tough time in the hockey. One Sunday afternoon Miss Jessie Riddell, in charge of Sunday School records, required the reason for seven new names suddenly appearing on our rolls with no parent reference.They were the members of St. Margaret's Sunday School, ( =a neighbouring church on Westminster St.) who had won the cup the previous year in Hockey, and they decided to play for Westminster. Following the phenomenal growth of the Sunday School other organizations commenced a successful run of operations. Youth and Men’s clubs became active — the enthusiastic Bible class of Mr. John A. McAulay, Stan Jones and the Actimist Club — the large enrolment of a public speaking men’s club gathered from all over this city, drew  at one time 3000 listeners attending a session in the Sanctuary , the halls of the church and the Tivioli Theatre (across the street) — a selected group of young men who spoke in churches in Brandon, Neepawa, Gladstone and other places. It has been my privilege to represent Westminster at Presbytery, Manitoba Conference, The World Congress of Sunday School  Workers held in Toronto (where we had a delegation of seven), at A.O.T.S. conventions in Vancouver, Banff, Kingston and Halifax which we also enjoyed, and we have had a busy, happy time. We have benefitted greatly in having the opportunity of association with the fine people of Westminster, and we have been proud of our church wherever visits were made. Looking backward the outstanding things in all the years have been the association with  the Boys’ Department for many years, and later the 15 years editing the Westminster Newsletter.
