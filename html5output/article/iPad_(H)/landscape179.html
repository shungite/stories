They have remained in Winnipeg and went on to be excellent Canadian citizens. The Refugee Committee was recreated again in April, 2014. Phyllis Reader was the chair and Val McIntyre was her assistant. (Val at left)They created an application form for “family reunification” sponsorship with Westminster United Church. The rationale was the church would not be financially responsible for the refugee but the requesting applicant would take full responsibility for their family member. The Betty Halstead Refugee Fund would act as a slush fund if needed. There were several applicants. Phyllis and Val did interviews with recorded notes. In all cases the applicants proved not to be appropriate for the mandate of the committee. The committee is on hold at the present time due to the financial and ministerial circumstances of the church. This report is written for the sole purpose of information and archival material on Refugee Sponsorships at Westminster United Church over the years. —with our thanks to Phyllis Reader who provided this text. Westminster Young Men’s Club Some things are hard to pin down. For example: when did the Young Men's Club get started? We have a report from 1912 (E.B Dearmond, President and T.S. Hamilton, Secretary) which suggests that the Young Men’s Club had been in existence for some time — “A number of new members have been added, and the old ones knit more closely together …” That year they had a Valentine Social. They had a debate with St. Andrew’s Church Club-topic: “Should Canada own and operate a navy?” Dr. Wilson of Augustine Church gave a lecture on Egypt “with lime-light views.”
