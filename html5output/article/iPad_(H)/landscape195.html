Stories of Westminster United Church & its People / Page 195
1)Early in 1998 a sub-committee was created from our Outreach Committee with the mandate of looking into refugee sponsorships. In the spring of 1998 I met with Despic Mladen and his wife at the church. After discussion it was decided the church would look at sponsoring his cousin, Lela Saric and her family to Winnipeg. Lela had a husband and 3 daughters. They were trapped in Sarajevo, Yugoslavia during the civil war in that country. After discussion with the Outreach Committee and the Board of the Church a motion was put forth to sponsor this family. A letter was sent the United Church head office in Toronto. On April 21, 1998 a Letter of Approval was received from the United Church. It was forwarded to Immigration Canada. On Sept. 3, 1999 a letter was received from Citizenship and Immigration that they had been approved and would be arriving in Winnipeg on Sept.21, 1999. This was a happy day. Lela, her husband, Zeljko and daughters, Violeta, Jovana and Danijela arrived safely and were greeted with family members. With Betty Halsteadâ€™s efforts of fund raising we raised over $10,000.00. There was a lot of interest and support from the congregation. This family has remained in Winnipeg and became employed with all the girls receiving their education here in the city. This was a successful sponsorship. 2) I received a letter in the church office on April 2, 2000 from an Allahyar Raza. I interviewed him and his request from the church was to sponsor his brother, Khalil Raza who was a teenager trapped in Moscow, Russia. He had been separated from his family during the Afghanistan war. Allahyar was prepared to support his brother financially, emotionally and culturally. He wanted the documentation done by Westminster United Church as Immigration Canada would not accept his application as he was a student at University of Winnipeg. The Outreach Committee and the Board of the Church accepted this application and it went through the process with Immigration Canada. Due to the 9/11 attacks in New York City in 2001 there were numerous delays. On Sept. 29th, 2003 Khalil Raza arrived at the Winnipeg airport to the absolute joy of his brother, Allahyar. The church gave him $500.00 for clothing and some miscellaneous items to get started. He quickly became employed and excelled in his new home. He is married and now lives in Vancouver, B.C. Again a very successful sponsorship.
