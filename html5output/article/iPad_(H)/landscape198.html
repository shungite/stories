In the early twenties many of the large homes in the vicinity of Westminster were occupied by our members and their large families. The result was our Sunday School had a large attendance and at one time we had the largest Sunday School enrolment of any Presbyterian Church in Canada. We had a boys’ department of around 150 boys between 12 and 17. This Department had been well organized by the late Walter MacDonald and he had a powerful executive which met frequently on plans and projects — Colonel J.D. Sinclair, T.D. Patton, M. McCracken, B.V. Richardson, Wm. Bonellie, J. R. Major, and I was included as I was running the gym classes twice a week.  Class space was hard to find. Two classes in the kitchen, 6 or 7 on the gym floor (Lower Hall), one each in the hall, the men's washroom, the vestry, and the  shower dressing room ( the latter usually erupted  in weekly disaster). Wm. Hurst was assistant on records and he usually caused an upset when coming in weeding through the classes wearing a large racoon coat. Curriculum teachers’ book were poor and teachers hard to find. We had four religious denominations teaching at a time. We followed the Tuxis and Trail Ranger badge award systems and several times we held annual Bible examinations. This was apparently unique as Manitoba College (a Presbyterian college) sent over prof. Dr. Fred Kerr and one other to look us over. We had over 100 boys take part. One afternoon one class had a sound recorder take a record of pupils taking part in the lesson. One lad had the part of repeating the Lord's Prayer; he had been interrupted , lost the trend and could not continue. Dr. Christie (Westminster’s third Minister) happened to drop in so we gave him a demonstration of class working. When he listened to a boy who could not repeat the Lord’s Prayer (on the recording)  he demanded the name of the parents so he could call on them. The culprit was his youngest son! Competition in indoor and outdoor athletics between our groups and other churches in the city was keen. Churches did all this in early days. Community Clubs and schools did little; in fact few Community Clubs existed. Sunday School leagues had a large organization take care of athletic meets, swim meets, basketball, hockey, soccer, lacrosse for the entire city. Westminster generally did very well and some photos are in our church vestibule.  Winning medals
