•     That same year, 1925,  on the 10th of June, the Presbyterian, Methodist and Congregationalist       churches entered into union and the United Church of Canada was formed. On Sunday, the 21st,       a congregational meeting was held during our morning service. The motion to concur with the       new union was made by Fred Harstone (picture). That motion wasn’t voted on till later in       the meeting. A motion was then moved by Mr. McGillvary to hold a vote ( on the intent of       Mr. Harstone’s motion) which was carried unanimously. The roll of the congregation was then       placed on the communion table. The names of those appointed to call the roll were agreed to.       Scrutineers were then appointed. The roll was then called to which 569 voted in favour, and       129 voted against. The Chairman, Dr. Christie, declared the motion (Mr. Harstone’s) to concur       in Union carried. Later 124 members of the congregation left Westminster. David Christie       and Fred Harstone were appointed members of the first General Council of the United Church.       Christie was also the first Chair of Winnipeg Presbytery. •     1927  / On June 19th, after Evening Service ended at 8:30, a Young People’s Temperance League       rally was held at Knox Church to which our young people were invited. It should be noted that       during Christie’s time Temperance Pledge Cards were from time to time handed out to our Sunday       School members for their signatures.The week after the Knox Church event we took up a special       collection to support a Temperance campaign concerning the plebiscite which had to do with the       proposed extension of the sale of beer. In Rev. Allan Huband’s time, the1950s, we were still       concerned about extending opportunities to purchase alcoholic beverages in Manitoba. •     In 1927 the Church Directory mentioned a Clerk Of Session, a Clerk of the Deacons’ Court,       and the various other officers (directors and presidents of mission groups) mentioned elsewhere.       There was also a Sewing Circle which met regularly on Thursday afternoons (which, some time       later was replaced by a Quilting Circle), and Choir Practice was at 8:00 PM every Friday. •     1929  / In the June 2nd Bulletin David Christie was listed as Minister, and G. Campbell Wadsworth|       was listed as Assistant Minister. That Sunday morning John Sutherland Bonnell was the Guest       Speaker. It was announced in the bulletin “The Session of Westminster Church hereby calls a       meeting on June 24th next … to consider Dr. Christie’s resignation … and to receive the Session’s       recommendation regarding a Successor.” The successor was to be John Sutherland Bonnell.
