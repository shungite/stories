Moving along in the sermon Bonnell covers the French Revolution with Robespierre and the “crimson tide that flowed from the place of execution.” He tells (referring to Thomas Carlyle’s History of The French Revolution ) how a famous opera singer of the day“ a woman fair to look upon when well rouged” was placed upon the high alter of Notre Dame Cathedral in Paris “while, in the midst of the cathedral, the frantic mob danced the fierce carmagnole until they fell in delirium and exhaustion.” The opera singer was “the goddess of Reason,” France had done with religion, God was banished from the land of “liberty, equality, fraternity.” The bulk of the sermon is a direct and implacable condemnation of the Communists and the Communist government. Bonnell had visited Russia. He tells what he saw— crosses torn down and images of Lenin places on altars, thousands of churches closed, religious education banned, seminaries for the training of Orthodox priests closed, atheism taught to children from kindergarten on, church services limited to masses and prayers (no homilies), evangelical preachers killed or thrown out of Russia. The list goes on. But, all is not lost, he says. “God is still on the throne. Millions of Russians continue to worship him I have not the slightest fear of the ultimate fate of religion in Russia. Few people in the world are so spiritual.” What then was Bonnell’s fear? He continues … “… In many cases church attendance on the part of a worker will prove to be a serious obstacle in the way of promotion in his employment. It prohibits his playing any part in the government of the country. … Already in this nation, there are many organizations working for the overthrow of our faith. The Union of Militant Atheists of Russia is a member of an international organization called “The International of the Proletarian Free Thinkers.” … that carries on its atheistic propaganda in various countries of the world including the United States. In our nation there is a group of people among its ministers and its religious educators who believe that Communists are not seriously bent upon the extermination of religion; that Christian churches and organizations ought freely to cooperate with them and thereby win their good will; that if the Christian Church and its ministers and educators would but take some of the slogans of communism
Rev. John Sutherland Bonnell
