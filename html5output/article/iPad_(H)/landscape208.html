There is a sense in which we are still too close to our years at Westminster to get a proper perspective on the life and work of the congregation. Suffice it to say that in the midst of all the programs and special events that we have touched on in these last dozen pages, my own time was naturally taken up most of all with Sunday worship and pastoral care. There were many demands on one’s time: hospital visiting, as much home visiting as it was humanly possible to do, funerals, weddings, counselling, many long days and many late nights. We were blessed with very dedicated and competent staff associates, and a vast array of just as dedicated and competent lay workers. As pointed out earlier, the new board structure made it very easy to bring new people into active participation. The nominating committee was always eager to have new names passed on to them, and in my pastoral visiting. I kept an eye open for any likely candidate for a spot on the General Board. These were great years, and retirement age closed in all too quickly. But, as someone commented at our retirement party, the term should not be “re-tired,” but merely “re-treaded” for future work. It certainly turned out that way! Perhaps a brief review of our own family should be given here, for during this sixteen-year tenure at Westminster, many things had happened.
