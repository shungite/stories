The other obstacle had been, that she had been trying to enlist Bob on this new adventure, but with no idea of where she was going or for how long she would be there, Bob was reluctant. He had other thing he wanted to do with his retirement. Finally, at Christmas time in 2004, Lorraine made up her mind that 2005 was going to be the year, and in a matter of a week or two, through a friend of a friend, she was given the name of Dwight DeLong, an American who has started a foundation in Romania for caring for orphans. She emailed him and asked if he had any volunteer work for a 59 year old woman who just wanted to cuddle babies. He said come ahead, and that she did. Another woman from the church asked if she could join the expedition, and plans moved so quickly for the two of them, that Lorraine’s passport arrived on a Friday, just in time for her Monday departure. She flew from Kelowna to Vancouver to Frankfurt to Budapest in Hungary, where she was picked up by a van, and driven just three hours, over the Romanian border to Oradea. When Lorraine and Betty arrived in Oradea, they discovered that, in fact, caring for babies under 2 years of age, was a new undertaking for the Foundation, and much of the two months they were there, was spent orienting the Romanian workers to this new undertaking. They worked on a floor of the children’s hospital in Oradea which housed babies up to the age of two years. The great majority of these babies had been born to Roma (gypsy) parents. The Roma custom is for the women to marry very young, and so they have large families, born into extreme poverty. The work of the foundation was to help the babies develop socially and physically so that they were healthy in more than a medical sense, as they moved back home, or into foster homes, and they had some degree of success.
