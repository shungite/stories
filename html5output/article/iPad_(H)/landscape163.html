Everyone was invited to indulge in a great variety of homemade delicacies — all for a nominal fee which went back to the Church coffers Reverend Christie, wrote of his first winter at the church in 1909 — “it was the hey day of immigration in Winnipeg. Sometimes rooming houses were not too attractive. To linger on the streets on the severe winter evenings was to invite frozen ears. If the church was a mecca for the faithful it was also a welcome refuge for the careless. Indeed on Sunday evenings the entire width of Hargrave Street was black with the stream of people, padding almost noiselessly over the snow, making their way to Knox or Central or First Baptist or Westminster. Groups could be seen, scurrying from one side to the other to these over crowded sanctuaries.” (From Winnipeg’s Great War: A City Comes of Age  by Jim Blanchard.) Women have always played an integral role in the life of the church. From as early as the nineteenth century, women worked as teachers, missionaries, and nurses, providing care in communities. As time went on, fundraising and social function organizing were also added to the women’s roles. When the United Church officially formed in 1925, the Ladies Aid (LA) (later the Woman’s Association (WA) and the Women’s Missionary Society (WMS) were doing work at home and abroad by Presbyterian and Methodist women. The WA focused on supporting the local church and community, while the WMS was more involved with education and world mission. In this period Westminster continued its work with missions at home and abroad. The Westminster Ladies Aid Society, for example, financed a hospital in Eriksdale, which opened in May 1926. Annual Reports show the important task of home visits and visits to hospitals done by the women during those early years of Westminster Church as well as financial support. From the 1923 Annual Report: “The Church greatly appreciated the offer of the Ladies Society to aid us with our finances by giving to us the interest on Bonds they hold together with an additional 10% of all the monies collected by them during the year. When in November Westminster Church asked for a special collection for its budget, our Society relinquished its claim on the offering of the Ladies Society and presented it, together with an added donation from our own funds, to the special collection of the church.” There were 1172 visits made to patients in hospitals.
