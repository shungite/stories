When Lorraine and Betty arrived in Oradea, they discovered that, in fact, caring for babies under 2 years of age, was a new undertaking for the Foundation, and much of the two months they were there, was spent orienting the Romanian workers to this new undertaking. They worked on a floor of the children’s hospital in Oradea which housed babies up to the age of two years. The great majority of these babies had been born to Roma (gypsy) parents. The Roma custom is for the women to marry very young, and so they have large families, born into extreme poverty. The work of the foundation was to help the babies develop socially and physically so that they were healthy in more than a medical sense, as they moved back home, or into foster homes, and they had some degree of success. Dwight did distance work for a computer software development firm in Washington State, and 80% of his salary went to pay Romanians who worked for the foundation. When Lorraine and her friend Betty returned to Canada, one of their goals was to raise enough money to supplement Dwight’s salary, so that the work they were doing as volunteers, could be taken over by Romanian workers. Churches, service clubs, and individuals in our area helped them largely reach that goal. In 2006, Lorraine went back to her babies in Romania. She spent over a month there in May — this time alone — but she was really happy to see the unbelievable difference that one year had made in the reactions of the babies. She said that she would never had thought of adopting any of the babies she saw the previous year, because they would all have psychological problems resulting from neglect. But in one year, the fact that babies were being held to be fed, cuddled and played with, meant that they seemed like normal healthy children who would grow up with a future! She also marvelled at the difference in the hospital personnel. The results of this program that they had witnessed, greatly influenced their attitudes, so that they started treating the babies as people, rather than seeing the hospital as a warehouse to shelter them. Finally, Lorraine was also pleased to see how the money which had been collected here in the Okanagan was providing salaries for good Romanian workers who were making all the difference to the work there. Dwight said that they had become one of the Foundation’s most important sources of funding!
