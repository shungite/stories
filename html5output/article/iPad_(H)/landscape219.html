Miss Marion Forbes had been with us since 1950, moving along as part of our family and household from Edmonton to Regina to Winnipeg. But, the time came when she didn’t want to be alone in the house when we were away, and in 1968, with Fran’s wedding in London in the offing, she moved into the very fine (and new) Fred Douglas Lodge. We were always in close touch with her, and she was back to the house for special events, as her health permitted. Following an attack of pneumonia in the summer of 1970, she had to be moved to a nursing home for a month or two. I was with her when she died peacefully on October 2, 1970. She had been an important part of our whole family, and we were always grateful that our home could be her home for twenty years. During the late sixties, Dorothy interspersed many church activities with the completion of five courses at the University of Manitoba. That gave her the qualifications to be a teacher-librarian, and from 1969–1979, first part-time, then full-time, she was on the Laura Secord Elementary School staff. With an honours BA from Queen’s, a BEd from the University of Alberta, and the library course at U of M, she well merited a special Dorothy Vipond retirement party at Laura Secord. Mary had gone to Queen’s on a General Motors scholarship while we were in Regina, and her graduation in 1964 coincided with our twenty-fifth wedding anniversary. She did her MA in history at the University of Toronto, and worked for a year and a half editing the foreign trade newsletter for the Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce. But she soon recognized that she wanted an academic life, so enrolled in a PhD program at U of T. She was a teaching assistant for a year at Scarborough College, then joined the history staff at Loyola in Montreal (later amalgamated with Sir George Williams to become Concordia University.) Completing her PhD after a couple of years teaching, she has continued on staff there. She and Bill Butler were married on October 9, 1977, at their home in NDG, and are the proud parents of Peter, born August 25, 1978, and Catherine, born May 26, 1980. Bill is also a grad of Queen’s (1964) and has an MA from the University of Toronto (1965). He teaches mathematics in a CEGEP in Montreal.
