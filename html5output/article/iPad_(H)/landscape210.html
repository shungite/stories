There were many demands on one’s time: hospital visiting, as much home visiting as it was humanly possible to do, funerals, weddings, counselling, many long days and many late nights. We were blessed with very dedicated and competent staff associates, and a vast array of just as dedicated and competent lay workers. As pointed out earlier, the new board structure made it very easy to bring new people into active participation. The nominating committee was always eager to have new names passed on to them, and in my pastoral visiting. I kept an eye open for any likely candidate for a spot on the General Board. These were great years, and retirement age closed in all too quickly. But, as someone commented at our retirement party, the term should not be “re-tired,” but merely “re-treaded” for future work. It certainly turned out that way! Perhaps a brief review of our own family should be given here, for during this sixteen-year tenure at Westminster, many things had happened. Miss Marion Forbes had been with us since 1950, moving along as part of our family and household from Edmonton to Regina to Winnipeg. But, the time came when she didn’t want to be alone in the house when we were away, and in 1968, with Fran’s wedding in London in the offing, she moved into the very fine (and new) Fred Douglas Lodge. We were always in close touch with her, and she was back to the house for special events, as her health permitted. Following an attack of pneumonia in the summer of 1970, she had to be moved to a nursing home for a month or two. I was with her when she died peacefully on October 2, 1970. She had been an important part of our whole family, and we were always grateful that our home could be her home for twenty years. During the late sixties, Dorothy interspersed many church activities with the completion of five courses at the University of Manitoba. That gave her the qualifications to be a teacher-librarian, and from 1969–1979, first part-time, then full-time, she was on the Laura Secord Elementary School staff. With an honours BA from Queen’s, a BEd from the University of Alberta, and the library course at U of M, she well merited a special Dorothy Vipond retirement party at Laura Secord.
