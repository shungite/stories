Unit 10, too, is still going strong, celebrating their 50 year mark as a unit within the church, Phyllis Reader reports. They are going to the Temple Gardens Spa in Moose Jaw to celebrate this auspicious occasion at the end of the month! Unit 10 meets once a month during the winter months and has a special dinner at Christmas and spring. Liz Witjkamp, Rita Menzies, Val McIntyre, Lynn Heise and Ann Peeler have been members since the beginning. Phyllis goes on to say the group “was a great support, as members went through the various stages of life, career changes, raising kids, grandchildren, weddings, funerals and parents passing away. We did our share of teas and helping with after church luncheons, garage sales, and contributions of home baking for a host of events. We've been through it all and have developed very strong, loyal relationships over the years. We met in members homes once a month. Discussed church business and all of us have served on the various committees and the church board. In fact they are now recycling us again.” Women were part of many activities of the Church, singing in the Choir, teaching Sunday School and leading youth groups, such as Canadian Girls in Training. One of the groups of women that has continued over most of Westminster’s 125 years is the Sewing Circle, becoming the Quilting Group, and more recently, including a knitting group. Through many decades this group provided hundreds of quilts, layettes, afghans, knitted hats, mitts and scarves to those in need, all over the world. For example, in 1937, 25 quilts given to the needy, in 1942, 26quilts and 48 warm garments shipped overseas and another 6 quilts sent to fresh air camps. In 1943, 30 quilts and afghans sent to the Red Cross to be part of their victory bundles and 19 layettes and many knitted garments were distributed in Winnipeg. Westminster women sent their crafted items to Europe, Korea, Angola, and to small Northern communities in norther Manitoba, and locally to the Xmas cheer board, Stella Ave Mission and as close to home as Mulvey School. Recently, knitting became the activity of choice for many volunteers, working at home and in church. They have continued to produce mitts, scarves etc. for the Christmas season mitten tree. Another group of knitters was formed in 2005 to produce prayer shawls. A few historic names have been recorded of the women taking part in this activity over the years.
