Bert can tell his own story. The Annual Meeting he alludes to in the beginning took place in 1972 during the Ministry of Reid Vipond, the same year this document was written … “At the Annual Meeting of Westminster held in the Sanctuary this year Dr. Vipond was kind enough to draw attention to the fact that I had completed 53 years of activity in this church and invited the congregation to give suitable recognition. I am deeply grateful to our minister for making this reference. Fifty three years is a short period in life’s record and I have enjoyed every bit of it and am thankful that I had the opportunity of being associated with such fine people in serving the Master. When I arrived in Winnipeg in 1919 I made a habit of visiting various churches to determine what part of the city I would live in.I dropped into Westminster one September evening and was delighted to find that a Boys' Conference was being held and the speaker was Mr. Taylor Staten. He is the man who started Tuxis and Trail Rangers — a great friend of mine. He spotted me in the congregation and told the Conference the story of three lads from Calgary who came to the Lake of The Woods in 1910 to a Boys’ Conference. He related that two of them had given their lives in the First World War, and he had had a visit with the remaining one a few months earlier while lecturing to troops during the march of occupation in Germany; I had obtained leave and had travelled 20 kilometres to listen to my friend. Taylor Staten then asked me to come up to the pulpit and say a few words. Although that was my introduction to Westminster, I really had the Westminster table pinned on me many years before I ever heard of this church. The next evening a group of 12 year old lads called at my room in the Y and invited me to come as their teacher. An auspicious start. Westminster has always had grand ministers and very able associates. When pulpit supply was required, outstanding ministers or laymen fro all corners of the east preached.
