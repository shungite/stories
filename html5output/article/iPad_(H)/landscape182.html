One event each year was the congregational picnic at the parks. One year we hired a C.N.R. four or five coach train, sold tickets all over Winnipeg and spent the Saturday afternoon at Victoria Beach. They gave me $200.00 to buy prizes and we spent it all. When the races started every person or stranger at Victoria Beach turned up to get in on the races and the grand prize list. Senator John T. Haig acted as our policeman and saw to it that no-one took part unless they were connected with Westminster. In the early twenties many of the large homes in the vicinity of Westminster were occupied by our members and their large families. The result was our Sunday School had a large attendance and at one time we had the largest Sunday School enrolment of any Presbyterian Church in Canada. We had a boys’ department of around 150 boys between 12 and 17. This Department had been well organized by the late Walter MacDonald and he had a powerful executive which met frequently on plans and projects — Colonel J.D. Sinclair, T.D. Patton, M. McCracken, B.V. Richardson, Wm. Bonellie, J. R. Major, and I was included as I was running the gym classes twice a week.  Class space was hard to find. Two classes in the kitchen, 6 or 7 on the gym floor (Lower Hall), one each in the hall, the men's washroom, the vestry, and the  shower dressing room ( the latter usually erupted  in weekly disaster). Wm. Hurst was assistant on records and he usually caused an upset when coming in weeding through the classes wearing a large racoon coat. Curriculum teachers’ book were poor and teachers hard to find. We had four religious denominations teaching at a time. We followed the Tuxis and Trail Ranger badge award systems and several times we held annual Bible examinations. This was apparently unique as Manitoba College (a Presbyterian college) sent over prof. Dr. Fred Kerr and one other to look us over. We had over 100 boys take part. One afternoon one class had a sound recorder take a record of pupils taking part in the lesson. One lad had the part of repeating the Lord's Prayer; he had been interrupted , lost the trend and could not continue. Dr. Christie (Westminster’s third Minister) happened to drop in so we gave him a demonstration of class working.
