But just before the Appendix, I must relate what appeared in the Westminster Church calendar on my last Sunday, and this actually found its way into a September 1979 issue of PUNCH magazine in England. I had given the church secretary the general outline for the calendar material, including hymns, lessons, sermon subject, etc. And then I said, “Why not indicate that it’s my last Sunday at Westminster?” Later in the week, the organist phoned in the music for Sunday, including the titles for prelude, offertory and postlude. When the calendar appeared, this is how it read:           Sunday. June 24, 1979           Dr. Vipond’s last Sunday at Westminster           Organ Prelude: “Now Thank We All Our God” Apparently, someone from Britain was visiting Westminster that day and showed PUNCH the calendar! Is there such a thing as a Freudian slip? The case rests! The Westminster Children's Care Centre Inc The Westminster Children's Care Centre was established in 1965 in answer to a real and urgent need in the community served by Westminster Church.Under the leadership of Dr. George Sisler  the agency was chartered under the Manitoba Companies Act as an independent,  non-profit, non- sectarian, organization existing primarily to conduct a day-care program for the children of working mothers. A Committee was formed with Mr. Justice James (Jimmy) Wilson as the Chair, of the committee. Officers were: Bob Peeler, Vice chair (later-Chair); Tom Smith, Treasurer; Trudy Perkins, Director; and Lorraine Perkins, Assistant Director. Other members of the Committee (as of 1973): M. Leithead, M. Armstrong, C. Lamont, R. Friesen, M. Brown, H. Badger, I. Lees and Dr. Murray Campbell. Originally the Centre was open to children (Grades 1 to 6) of single moms.
