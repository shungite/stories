Westminster Young Men’s Club Some things are hard to pin down. For example: when did the Young Men's Club get started? We have a report from 1912 (E.B Dearmond, President and T.S. Hamilton, Secretary) which suggests that the Young Men’s Club had been in existence for some time — “A number of new members have been added, and the old ones knit more closely together …” That year they had a Valentine Social. They had a debate with St. Andrew’s Church Club-topic: “Should Canada own and operate a navy?” Dr. Wilson of Augustine Church gave a lecture on Egypt “with lime-light views.” The Club also erected a memorial window, dedicated on June, 1912 to W.J. Stafford. We still enjoy that beautiful window, located in the Narthex (entrance vestibule) of the Church. The picture depicts the story of David’s escape from the King who regarded him as competitor. The King’s son, Jonathon (with the bow and arrow) was a very close friend of David and betrayed his father to help his friend. The love these two young men had for each other is recounted in the First book of Samuel, Chapter 20. The Young Men’s Club held an annual picnic on July first in conjunction with the Young Ladies Club. The Men's prowess in sports is reported in an article on sports in this eBook.
