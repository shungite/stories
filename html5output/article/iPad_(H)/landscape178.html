When the United Church officially formed in 1925, the Ladies Aid (LA) (later the Woman’s Association (WA) and the Women’s Missionary Society (WMS) were doing work at home and abroad by Presbyterian and Methodist women. The WA focused on supporting the local church and community, while the WMS was more involved with education and world mission. In this period Westminster continued its work with missions at home and abroad. The Westminster Ladies Aid Society, for example, financed a hospital in Eriksdale, which opened in May 1926. Annual Reports show the important task of home visits and visits to hospitals done by the women during those early years of Westminster Church as well as financial support. From the 1923 Annual Report: “The Church greatly appreciated the offer of the Ladies Society to aid us with our finances by giving to us the interest on Bonds they hold together with an additional 10% of all the monies collected by them during the year. When in November Westminster Church asked for a special collection for its budget, our Society relinquished its claim on the offering of the Ladies Society and presented it, together with an added donation from our own funds, to the special collection of the church.” There were 1172 visits made to patients in hospitals. And the total givings amounted to $1,673.21. In 1925, at the time of Union, it is noted about one quarter of the missionary work of the United Church was supported by the women of the Women’s Missionary Society The records show that in 1928, the Women’s Auxiliary of Westminster had a membership of 78, and contributed $1,800 to the work of The Women’s Missionary Society. There were 32 in the Mission Band that year, that contributed $200.00. I wanted to share this motto of the Women’s Missionary Society from those early years: “The World for Christ Not by might nor by power, but by my spirit, saith the Lord of Hosts. Whatsoever He saith unto you, do it.” It was the ‘Do It’ that spoke to me, and continues to speak to Westminster women.
