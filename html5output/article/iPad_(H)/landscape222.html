Then, under the Day Care Act , 1986 , it was operated with a Board of Directors, a formal budget a Director and Staff under a set of rules and regulations.  Church people were still involved both creating lunches and snacks, and with volunteers meeting the kids from school to lunch at the church and back again and again at close of school day. Volunteers also sat with them and chatted during lunch hours. In 1995 it was incorporated as a Government agency. The Centre can handle 50 children. Twelve spaces are reserved for children ages 4 and 5. They come to the Centre before school (nursery and Kindergarten)  in the morning, have breakfast and are then taken to school by staff. They are then picked up by stafff at 11:30, returned to the Centre and given lunch. They then stay at the Centre till they are picked up by their parents after work. There are 38 spaces for children ages 6 to 12. They have the same regimen morning and noon but return to school for the afternoon. Then they are picked up at the end of school day and returned to the Centre where they are picked up by their parents. As a government agency  the Centre now operates full time (during school holidays and summer vacation). According to staff the numbers of children attending varies during these "holiday" times but generally maintains the same level of attendance all year. There are no more volunteers and therefore no more direct participation of the Westminster congregation in the operation. The total paid staff under Director Greg Blanco, is trained and professional. The  Staff sets a monthly meal plan (based on Canada food guide), does the shopping for groceries, and prepares and serves breakfast, lunch and after school snacks. The cost of the food program is approximately $13,000/annum . Food is prepared i the Church's basement kitchen and the children eat in the area we know as our gymnasium. The program offered to the children is Government stipulated; "Children should learn through play" at the Centre.
