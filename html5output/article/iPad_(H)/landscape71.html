Stories of Westminster United Church & its People / Page 71
Mission is as alive in Westminster now as in David Christie’s time. It just looks different. Twenty-first Century different. We no longer send people out to the Third World to proselytize for Jesus. Our money and our personal efforts are directed at meeting people’s needs (as they express them) in the spirit of Jesus rather than in the interests of an institutional church. Here is a List of incredible and relentless giving and outreach in the past: 1   During the First World War The Ladies Society (which met regularly       on Thursday afternoons) produced the following materials:       • 1150 articles for the Red Cross.       • 1303 pairs hand-knitted sox (609 sent to “our own Westminster boys overseas”).       • 76 pairs hand-knitted mitts.       • 41 hand-knitted scarves.       • 85 trench caps.       • 225 Christmas parcels sent to our boys overseas.       • Plus: $25 voted for delicacies for convalescent soldiers in city hospitals. 2   After the War and during The Flu Epidemic the Ladies’ Society created, for Winnipeg hospitals,       bed linen, aprons and caps using material supplied by the hospitals. 3 The Congregation provided the whole support for (our) missionary in India Dorothy Kilpatrick.       (We also supported: Armenian and Syrian Relief, Milk River Mission Organ Fund, Jewish Mission,       Belgian Relief, etc). 2 A Report from our Files:       April, 1930, Fifth Anniversary of The United Church:       a) every 6th person in Canada is connected to the UC.       b) in Canada the UC has an army 1600 Home Missionaries.       c) in foreign countries we have 600 missionaries with 1100 native helpers.       d) in our Sunday Schools we have 620,000 scholars.
