OVERVIEW OF REFUGEE SPONSORSHIPS IN WESTMINSTER UNITED CHURCH 1)Early in 1998 a sub-committee was created from our Outreach Committee with the mandate of looking into refugee sponsorships. In the spring of 1998 I met with Despic Mladen and his wife at the church. After discussion it was decided the church would look at sponsoring his cousin, Lela Saric and her family to Winnipeg. Lela had a husband and 3 daughters. They were trapped in Sarajevo, Yugoslavia during the civil war in that country. After discussion with the Outreach Committee and the Board of the Church a motion was put forth to sponsor this family. A letter was sent the United Church head office in Toronto. On April 21, 1998 a Letter of Approval was received from the United Church. It was forwarded to Immigration Canada. On Sept. 3, 1999 a letter was received from Citizenship and Immigration that they had been approved and would be arriving in Winnipeg on Sept.21, 1999. This was a happy day. Lela, her husband, Zeljko and daughters, Violeta, Jovana and Danijela arrived safely and were greeted with family members. With Betty Halstead’s efforts of fund raising we raised over $10,000.00. There was a lot of interest and support from the congregation.
