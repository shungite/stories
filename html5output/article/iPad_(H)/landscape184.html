We had four religious denominations teaching at a time. We followed the Tuxis and Trail Ranger badge award systems and several times we held annual Bible examinations. This was apparently unique as Manitoba College (a Presbyterian college) sent over prof. Dr. Fred Kerr and one other to look us over. We had over 100 boys take part. One afternoon one class had a sound recorder take a record of pupils taking part in the lesson. One lad had the part of repeating the Lord's Prayer; he had been interrupted , lost the trend and could not continue. Dr. Christie (Westminster’s third Minister) happened to drop in so we gave him a demonstration of class working. When he listened to a boy who could not repeat the Lord’s Prayer (on the recording)  he demanded the name of the parents so he could call on them. The culprit was his youngest son! Competition in indoor and outdoor athletics between our groups and other churches in the city was keen. Churches did all this in early days. Community Clubs and schools did little; in fact few Community Clubs existed. Sunday School leagues had a large organization take care of athletic meets, swim meets, basketball, hockey, soccer, lacrosse for the entire city. Westminster generally did very well and some photos are in our church vestibule.  Winning medals were provided in all leagues by the Griffon Club of Winnipeg to all sections from Midget to Senior. We had a tough time in the hockey. One Sunday afternoon Miss Jessie Riddell, in charge of Sunday School records, required the reason for seven new names suddenly appearing on our rolls with no parent reference.They were the members of St. Margaret's Sunday School, ( =a neighbouring church on Westminster St.) who had won the cup the previous year in Hockey, and they decided to play for Westminster. Following the phenomenal growth of the Sunday School other organizations commenced a successful run of operations. Youth and Men’s clubs became active — the enthusiastic Bible class of Mr. John A. McAulay, Stan Jones and the Actimist Club — the large enrolment of a public speaking men’s club gathered from all over this city, drew  at one time 3000 listeners attending a session in the Sanctuary , the halls of the church and the Tivioli Theatre (across the street) — a selected group of young men who spoke in churches in Brandon, Neepawa, Gladstone and other places.
