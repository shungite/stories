Joan McConnell went with Barry to Britain for a year’s sabbatical in 1978, and Elaine Hickerson, wife of Rev. Bill Hickerson, secretary of Manitoba conference, came as her replacement. Audrey McLennan also left in 1978 to work full- time at her home church, Fort Garry, and Orma Backhouse replaced her. Partly as a footnote, and as a point of interest, Joan McConnell is now personnel secretary of Manitoba conference. Audrey McLennan later became president of Manitoba conference, and now chairs the department at head office that produces Spirit Connection  on Vision TV. The music department saw fewer changes than any other part of the staff. Herb Sadler was organist and choir director from 1919–1953. Glen Harrison then assumed the position of choir director—and is still there! Barry Anderson was organist for several years, and was succeeded in 1967 by Don Menzies, who is also still in that position. With a fine, four-manual Casavant organ, and excellent organists and choir directors, Westminster has had superlative music throughout its long history. In our years, Glen Harrison directed many special choir recitals, and on numerous occasions had a half dozen Winnipeg Symphony players for orchestral accompaniment. Don Menzies, as well as guest organists, have played (and broadcast) many recitals. I have heard both Marcel Dupré and E. Power Biggs in recital on that outstanding instrument. Writing this much about staff, I’ll also add a word here about our custodians. When we were first at Westminster, Mr. Jake Flipse looked after the church. A native of Holland, he had worked with a landscape gardening company when he first came to Canada. I’m not sure when he came to the church, but Mr. and Mrs. Flipse occupied the three-room apartment on the fourth floor of the church hall for many years. Mr. Flipse was quiet, unassuming, very kind, very efficient, and, of course, extremely capable in terms of the church lawn. His hours were long; he was literally on call anytime, all the time, from when the doors opened in the morning until the last meeting was over at night. He had Monday evening and all day Tuesday off. His replacement for those times was old Mr. Fish. I was glad he was there, because it made possible my little joke: ours was the only church in town that employed Fish and Flipse!
