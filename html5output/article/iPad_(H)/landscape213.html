A Committee was formed with Mr. Justice James (Jimmy) Wilson as the Chair, of the committee. Officers were: Bob Peeler, Vice chair (later-Chair); Tom Smith, Treasurer; Trudy Perkins, Director; and Lorraine Perkins, Assistant Director. Other members of the Committee (as of 1973): M. Leithead, M. Armstrong, C. Lamont, R. Friesen, M. Brown, H. Badger, I. Lees and Dr. Murray Campbell. Originally the Centre was open to children (Grades 1 to 6) of single moms. Practically, it was open to the children of single parents, mothers or fathers of children at Mulvey School. It was open during the "school year", but not on holidays (Christmas, Easter and the Summer.) Then, under the Day Care Act , 1986 , it was operated with a Board of Directors, a formal budget a Director and Staff under a set of rules and regulations.  Church people were still involved both creating lunches and snacks, and with volunteers meeting the kids from school to lunch at the church and back again and again at close of school day. Volunteers also sat with them and chatted during lunch hours. In 1995 it was incorporated as a Government agency. The Centre can handle 50 children. Twelve spaces are reserved for children ages 4 and 5. They come to the Centre before school (nursery and Kindergarten)  in the morning, have breakfast and are then taken to school by staff. They are then picked up by stafff at 11:30, returned to the Centre and given lunch. They then stay at the Centre till they are picked up by their parents after work. There are 38 spaces for children ages 6 to 12. They have the same regimen morning and noon but return to school for the afternoon. Then they are picked up at the end of school day and returned to the Centre where they are picked up by their parents. As a government agency  the Centre now operates full time (during school holidays and summer vacation). According to staff the numbers of children attending varies during these "holiday" times
