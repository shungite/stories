The mortgage is just about paid off at this point (1992), and from now on, the conference rental will become church income for Westminster. Second, about 1965 I suggested that we should set up a foundation fund for the church. There were always people asking if there was some particular need toward which a memorial contribution could be made. Also, there were three or four small trust funds (not more than a total of $10,000–$15,000) and they were carried along year after year, reported at the annual meeting, but never used. No one quite knew what to do with them. It was my feeling that if there were a Foundation Fund properly set up and publicized, there would be contributions made to it. Perhaps some people might put it into their wills. I knew that other churches had such a fund, including Knox in Winnipeg which had the Duval Foundation, named for its first minister. After much discussion, the idea got off the ground. A constitution was drawn up. It went through the proper channels in the provincial government so that it was legally chartered. Within a couple of years, two or three $10,000–$15,000 bequests were given. When the manse was sold (for a mere $38,000 in those years), that money was put in. Except in unusual circumstances, only the interest was to be used, and, even then, only for special expenses. In our years, most of the very fine stained-glass windows were cleaned and re-leaded using interest from the Foundation Fund. By the time of my retirement in 1979, the foundation stood at well over $100,000. The 1991 Annual Report shows $300,000.
