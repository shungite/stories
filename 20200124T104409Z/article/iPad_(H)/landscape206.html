A search of all the Sunday bulletins from January through to that day shows there was no mention of Mercer’s intention to leave Westminster, no receptions or teas organized to say “good-bye.” Just a curt announcement on the day of his leaving. I ask the question “What happened? Why the coldness of our Minister's departing?” I haven’t been able to find the answer in the memories of any Church members surviving from that year. Whether or not we appreciated his work with us or whether there were “issues” that compelled him to take such an abrupt leaving we’ll probably never know. What we do know is that once he as back in Alberta for the rest of his career the public outpouring  of praise for his services, both to the Church and to other public institutions was unstinting as he took his leave for retirement. Nelson Mercer cared deeply for people and his Ministry gave testimony to this wherever he worked. R.D.   — In His Own Words R.D. (Bert) Macfarlane represents a significant portion of the congregation which has always been the back bone of a successful Church — dedicated volunteers; the tireless men and women who run our programs and shoulder responsibilities for — among many reasons — the joy of serving. R.D. worked  in our Sunday School and our Athletic program for young people. He edited a newsletter for many years. He also fathered a girl who became, like himself, a leader every bit as strong as her father; Marilyn Macfarlane-Huband. Bert can tell his own story. The Annual Meeting he alludes to in the beginning took place in 1972 during the Ministry of Reid Vipond, the same year this document was written … “At the Annual Meeting of Westminster held in the Sanctuary this year Dr. Vipond was kind enough to draw attention to the fact that I had completed 53 years of activity in this church and invited the congregation to give suitable recognition. I am deeply grateful to our minister for making this reference. Fifty three years is a short period in life’s record and I have enjoyed every bit of it and am thankful that I had the opportunity of being associated with such fine people in serving the Master.
