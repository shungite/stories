In another document, we read that “(Nelson) served as United Church Chaplain in The Dr. Vernor Fanning Care Center and The Alberta Children’s Hospital.” Even in his retirement he turned to ministering / connecting  where there were people in particular need. “The 1960s was a period of involvement in the whole social scene. A lot of us became involved with inter-faith resulting in a period of looking out into the community” (Nelson, referring to his involvement with the Calgary Inter-Faith Community Action Committee). The group formed, or was the catalyst and support for many important social service initiatives in the city including the Calgary Inter-Faith Food Bank, Calgary Inter-Faith Secondhand Store, Inter-Faith Housing, Inter-Faith Lacombe Centre, Inter-Faith Youth and Family Services, Calgary Drug Information Centre, the Old Y Centre, Connection Housing, and Calgary Urban Project Society. The Inter-Faith aspect of the organization drew on the interests in it of a Roman Catholic priest, a Jewish Rabbi, and Nelson, among representative members of several  faiths. In a newspaper article written on Nelson’s retirement, the following: “(Nelson’s departure) … will leave a void not only in the United Church, but also in the Calgary Interfaith Organization … the past 17 years has contributed much to the spiritual welfare of Calgarians …” Some concluding data and thoughts During his time with Westminster Nelson was married to Roberta (Margaret Goodfellow), whom he had married in 1939, in Medicine Hat. They had five children; Joan, Joyce, Robert, John, and Harry. At least two members of the present Congregation remembered going to school with the girls. Roberta died in March of 1968. In 1970 Nelson then married Clara Robertson in 1970. Nelson died, after a long illness, in 1991. Clara lived until recently, 2016. One further note: On Sunday, October 25, 1962 a brief note appeared in our Sunday bulletin: “This will be Rev. Mercer’s final service at Westminster. A reception will be held this evening, after Service.”
