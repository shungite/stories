These camps flourished for much of the 20th century. The United Church from 1926 on was closely involved with our camps supervised by Manitoba and Northwestern Conference. This organization continued till 1967 when camp supervision was turned over to the Winnipeg Presbytery. By the 1970s changing social conditions and activities meant difficulties for the camps in terms of financeing and sometimes finding people to use them. In the end some camps along the west side of Lake Winnipeg closed, some were sold to government to become vacation camps. Across Canada the United Church continues to this day in supporting camps. However, again, financing and lack of interest in attending camps has taken its toll. Westminster’s 50-50 Clubs Feb 16/36: Notice “Westminster Young Women’s Club will hold their first Members Night on Tuesday commencing at 8:00 PM. An invitation is extended to all young men in the congregation.” From our earliest years to well into the 1950s and 60s our congregation gave birth to many clubs, mission and social groups which were important to our outreach work and which enriched the life of our church for many different ages and interests. For example a review of the records from the archives indicates that we had two 50-50 clubs (1938-46) (1947-53). The earliest group met for Christian fellowship and Bible Study on a monthly basis with an average attendance of 22 people. Subjects such as Child Psychology, Life in Nazi occupied Europe, Town Planning, a June picnic each year for children of the Protestant Orphans’ Home, Art, and the Christmas Story kept the members coming back and thereby making the club a success for a period of time. However, due to the movement of people to other interests, this portion of the 50-50 club dissolved in 1946 and later reconvened under different circumstances.*
