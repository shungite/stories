(“I’ve toured all of old Scotland,” she used to say. “Now, if I could only see New Scotland.”) The church’s farewell gift to her was airfare for her and Phil to Halifax, with a para-transit van waiting for them to do a two-week tour in the Maritimes. Unfortunately, upon arrival back in Winnipeg, she suffered a bout of pneumonia, and after being bed-ridden for a few weeks, the arthritis became so severe she had to spend her remaining year and a half in bed. After her death, Phil began a life of travel, revisiting countries where he had served in WWII. He had been a very patient, caring, and long-serving husband. Pat Hodges arrived in Winnipeg with her husband, Houston, just as the search committee was looking for a new church secretary. Houston was a United  Methodist minister from Texas, and had come to be the social action secretary of Winnipeg presbytery. Joan McConnell met the Hodges, discovered that Pat had been a church secretary in the deep south and was hoping to get employment in Winnipeg. It was a perfect fit, and for our last two years at Westminster, the phone was answered with a happy and cheery southern drawl. For many years, indeed, from its beginning in 1892, Westminster had a Session and a Deacon’s Court. The Deacon’s Court was something like an official board in that it consisted of all members of Session, plus people who were on the finance and property committee. There was no Committee of Stewards; this function was performed by the Deacon’s Court. Somewhere in Dr. Allen Huband’s tenure (1948–1955), the decision was made to follow the United Church Manual which called for a Session, a Committee of Stewards, and an Official Board. In general terms, the Session has oversight over things spiritual, the Stewards over things temporal, and the Official Board is a combination of these two (plus presidents of all organizations). Also, it has the power to act on behalf of the congregation. But there has always been a problem here. Why should we divide the spiritual and the temporal? Aren’t they all of a piece, really? Why should one particular group (the Stewards) do all the property work, and even more especially, all the financial decisions?
