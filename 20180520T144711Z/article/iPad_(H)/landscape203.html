Reverend Reid Vipond When our eighth minister, Reid Vipond, wrote his memoirs in 1992, chapter 15 was entitled Westminster Revisited (1963-1979). In this section of his book he gave us an incredibly detailed look at what and where we were as a congregation for those 16 years. For a man of 80 years of age his memory for detail was amazing. We present the whole chapter here (7800 words!) with pleasure  and we thank Reid’s daughter, Mary, for sharing it with us. Reid writes: We so thoroughly enjoyed the British television version of Brideshead Revisited , that I thought the heading ‘Westminster Revisited’ was at least somewhat appropriate for this chapter. We, too, found ourselves reliving our earlier years, 1946–1949, often reminiscing with parishioners about the glory days back in the forties when the church was packed every Sunday morning; when an average Sunday evening service had an attendance of three or four hundred; when there were between 100 and 150 in the Apex Young People’s group; when I organized the junior youth group (mostly Grade 11 and 12 young people) whose innovative programs were among the most imaginative I have ever seen. I’ll never forget the evening they conducted a funeral for “the church” — complete with a casket (borrowed from Mordue’s Funeral Home).
