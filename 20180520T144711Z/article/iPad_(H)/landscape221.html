The Westminster Children’s Care Centre Inc The Westminster Children’s Care Centre was established in 1965 in answer to a real and urgent need in the community served by Westminster Church.Under the leadership of Dr. George Sisler the agency was chartered under the Manitoba Companies Act as an independent,  non-profit, non-sectarian, organization existing primarily to conduct a day-care program for the children of working mothers. A Committee was formed with Mr. Justice James (Jimmy) Wilson as the Chair, of the committee. Officers were: Bob Peeler, Vice chair (later-Chair); Tom Smith, Treasurer; Trudy Perkins, Director; and Lorraine Perkins, Assistant Director. Other members of the Committee (as of 1973): M. Leithead, M. Armstrong, C. Lamont, R. Friesen, M. Brown, H. Badger, I. Lees and Dr. Murray Campbell. Originally the Centre was open to children (Grades 1 to 6) of single moms. Practically, it was open to the children of single parents, mothers or fathers of children at Mulvey School. It was open during the "school year", but not on holidays (Christmas, Easter and the Summer.) Then, under the Day Care Act , 1986 , it was operated with a Board of Directors, a formal budget a Director and Staff under a set of rules and regulations.  Church people were still involved both creating lunches and snacks, and with volunteers meeting the kids from school to lunch at the church and back again and again at close of school day. Volunteers also sat with them and chatted during lunch hours.
