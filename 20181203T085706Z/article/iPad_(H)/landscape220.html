After the summer, a pastoral relations committee was formed, but didn’t become very active until early 1979. They moved through the usual stages of meetings with presbytery representatives, setting out goals, looking at new directions. I was aware (as usually happens) of some of their plans, but kept out of the way as much as possible. However, by the end of May 1979, they had made their choice—Rev. Bob Thompson, associate with Bob Shanks at St. Andrew’s-Wesley in Vancouver. We had the usual happy retirement parties. At Manitoba conference, at the end of May, I received my pen-and-pencil set. (Some three years before, about three dozen sets had been purchased from a jewellery store that was going out of business; “It will save the conference committee the trouble of looking for retiree gifts each year,” Fred Douglas remarked.) There was a staff party for both the Conlys and us at Orma Backhouse’s cottage at Victoria Beach, and several other groups had parties, too. The big event, however, was the salad supper at the church, serving six to seven hundred people. Following the meal, a program was held in the church, with presentations of roses to Dorothy; for me, a Sunday school picture book done by all the Sunday school kids; a Yashica camera from the board; a choir picture, and lots of lighthearted music from the choir; music from some of the Manitoba Chamber Orchestra, with a card signed by all the orchestra members; and finally, for us, a cheque for $6,000 from the congregation. What a night! During the supper hour, members of the planning committee took Dorothy and me to every table, stopping just long enough to say hello. It was a thrill to be able to call practically every individual person by name—one advantage of a long pastorate. The next day we went down to Encore Travel and booked a three-week tour to Greece for September. It was a great finale to a fabulous sixteen years—actually sixteen years plus the three years as assistant. There were only two more weeks to go, and the days and nights were filled with luncheons and dinner parties hosted by so many kind folk. We completed plans to rent our house to Dr. Brian Bevan, a middle-aged English doctor who was soon to bring his bride to Canada, and they were happy to rent a completely furnished house for nine months while they decided where they might wish to build or buy.
