Such a service had never been conducted in Canada before.” Ernest Marshall Howse: The Writer People who attend university to earn a Bachelor of (you-name-it) degree, but who go no further (Masters degree, Doctorate degree) are spared the pain of fulfilling the requirement for each of these latter degrees — a Thesis. The Thesis can sometime be an arduous work extending over several years. Howse entered his doctoral program in 1932. It was two years later, in 1934, that he completed his thesis, a work of nearly of 298 pages (including the appendices). He was a hard worker, and a fiercely focused student. His Thesis on the Clapham Sect earned him a Doctor of Philosophy Degree at the University of Edinburgh in October 1934. It is a long read of a particular group of people in the Church of England in the 18th century who abhorred slavery in the British Empire. William Wilberforce was their leader and an Act of Parliament was their goal.
