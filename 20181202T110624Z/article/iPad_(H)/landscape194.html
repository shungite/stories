MEMBERSHIP DROPS IN CHURCH WOMEN The consensus of United Church Women congregational presidents is that “membership is dropping drastically” chiefly  due to the working woman and lack of interest, retiring presbyterial president, Mrs. C.S. Barker reported Wednesday. The problem of poor response and lack of interest within individual churches was mentioned in several other reports given at the presbyterial's sixth annual meeting in Young United Church. Membership last year was 5230. Figures for the previous year were not available … There is one conclusion to be drawn from this news report, six years after the United Church “heralded  a new and important step in the status of women” in the denomination. The fact was that our overall decline in the membership was already evident in 1962 and had been evident in the 50s. Were we being offered a “new role” for women in the hope it would stop the shrinking  in that half of our membership? Yes. To quote a UC document intended to create a positive spin on the plan, “All this must lead to a stronger and better church.” Rather it served as a distraction from something of which many of us in the United Church were not really aware. The Untied Church, like many Protestant denominations, wasn’t (still isn’t) ready to face the implications of a new world where overall change was happening at an accelerated rate.  And in some sectors, is now happening at a dizzying rate. But the United Church moves slowly; ever so slowly. At the beginning of the 20th century,  we note in an article elsewhere,  the concern in the Protestant churches ( especially the Presbyterian and Anglican denomination) that young men were deserting the church in their late teens. Not young women. Young men. And yet, with this evident loss to our numbers and loss to our potential male  leadership we persisted for over 50 years in leaving the whole central control of the church in the hands of men. We didn't think to include women in the important areas of concern.We simply worked harder to keep young men involved. Sports, fellowship. More sports. I think of my own home (time period: 1930-1950) where mother was never apprised of how much money my father was earning. He gave her an allowance to run the house. No questions asked.
