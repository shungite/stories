Stories of Westminster United Church & its People / Page 64
•     1919  / A committee of Session was struck to find an Assistant Minister to help Dr. Christie.       No suitable candidate was found but alternatively Christie approved the appointment of       a secretary to assist him. This assistance was already in place at Knox and other Winnipeg       churches. A Ministerial Assistant was not in place at Westminster till 1925. •   December 14th, 1919  / the Colours of the 100th Overseas Battalion of the Winnipeg Grenadier were “deposited in the church” by Lt.-Col J.B. Mitchell (a founding member of the congregation (picture). Mitchell was the Officer in command of the regiment when it went overseas. He stated that the flags had been deposited in Canterbury Cathedral during the war. These flags still grace our sanctuary. •   Also on January 21st 1919  saw the formation of the Westminster Young Mens (Social and literary) Club. Dr. Christie was a moving force in its establishment.       The Vice President of the club was a man named Stuart Garson — later to be Premier       of Manitoba — who happened to live at 183 Chestnut Street. •     In 1920  we granted Dr. Christie an 8 month leave of absence. Church records state that his health       had deteriorated due to the strain on the congregation during the war period including the loss       of his son. The influenza epidemic with people ill and dying and with the church closed for weeks       must have taken its own strain on David’s ministry. He returned in November in much better       health. The congregation presented the Christies with a new Ford Sedan and the minister, a robe. •     In May of 1923 our first deaconess Miss Margaret MacKinnon was appointed. •     In 1924 a new departure was the arrangement between our congregation and that of       Young Methodist Church to hold services jointly during July and August; a morning service at       Westminster, and an evening service at Young. This arrangement was continued into the 1970’s. •     1925  / We appointed of our first Assistant Minister, Rev. Gerald Rogers. Church records indicate       that he performed so well that his appointment was made permanent. As did the position.
