The Junior and Bantam teams both reached the City finals. § By the late 1950s the influence of Westminster Church in its congregation’s lives as a place for social and physical activity was waining. Community clubs and other civic activity sites were attracting attention. Swimming pools and bowling alleys were catering to people both “churched’ and otherwise. Public schools were placing more emphasis on Physical Training as we called it and that program would morph to continue as Physical Education to the present day. And who could resist the world of professional sport on radio and television? Furthermore, iIn the post WW II years the Protestant churches, especially English speaking, Protestant churches had long-since lost sight of the arrogant notion that their young men, minds formed by faith, and bodies formed by healthy activity would take over, run, and shape a greater Canada. Also, young women were, and would be more so, moving into some of those leadership roles once the sole province of men. For instance … Does anyone remember the time when radio stations started having women, not just men with their deep, serious, CBC voices, read the news? Were women’s voices right for the solemn business of relaying news events? It was an open question for awhile and then we all settled down. We had other things to think about. The reality of shrinking congregations and the presence of the atom bomb were two that come to mind. As a society we still worship the idea, if not always the practice, of building strong healthy bodies of both women and men. But not in churches and not with God as part of the process.
