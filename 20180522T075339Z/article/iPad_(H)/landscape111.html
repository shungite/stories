He wasn’t able to get the thesis published in England while they were there. But in 1952 Canadian historian, A.R.M. Lower helped make publication possible in Toronto. Ernest tells us that as these things go, it was immediately “very successful” and, he further notes, it was still a good seller in 1979. “I doubt if any other PhD thesis in my generation has kept a place on the open market for close to thirty years.” (A small. unsubstantiated boast!)
The thesis is now available online.
In late 1933 Ernest was invited to be the minister of the church at Hawick, Scotland. He didn’t accept, however, having an urge to return to North America. At the end of the year he received an invitation to become ( as noted earlier) a Pastor in the Presbyterian church in Beverly Hills, California. He and Esther returned to California in April of 1934. He was invited to preach at churches in Santa Monica, Los Angeles and Pasadena while the church that wanted him, had the opportunity to watch him in action. Finally, in May he preached in Beverly Hills and the congregation voted to engage his services. His work in Beverly Hills was an engagement with the Wealthy and Famous. Earnest is a wonderful name dropper. Alphonso Bell (Bel-Air estates) invited them to tea with actor George Aliss; George Hormel (meats), “Hormel was a warm friend of mine.” George bought Ernest his first clerical gown; Mary McCormack (daughter of Cyrus, inventor if the reaping machine.) was especially fond of him.“For the last months we were in Beverly Hills we went without fail after the Sunday morning service to the McCormick estate for mid-day lunch. Other ‘names:’ Joe E. Brown (comedian), his two sons went to Ernest’s Sunday school; Fred Astaire’s mother was a member of the congregation; so was Jack Oakie’s mother; Mary Pickford’s niece came to their young people’s group; Will Rogers was not a church member but was an earlier supporter of the church — Ernest tried to contact him for a fundraiser but Rogers was getting ready for his trip with Wiley Post. Rogers and Post were to die on that trip.
