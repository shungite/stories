Obviously, Ernest’s choosing to go to Union is a reflection on his own theological matrix. He was there for one year, became a close friend of Fosdick, wrote his thesis on 18th Century English Deists, was awarded the degree Master of Sacred Theology (S.T.M.) and fell in love with an intelligent and attractive girl from Pasadena California named Esther Black (who was taking her Masters degree from Columbia University). He and Esther travelled together at the end of term for a vacation to Pasadena and her home. While there, the family’s minister (a Presbyterian) invited Ernest to be the preacher for two Sundays during his summer vacation.. (Two years later this contact lead to an invitation for him to become the minister of the Presbyterian Church in Beverly Hills.) After summer vacation Ernest went back to NFLD to visit his parents before heading to Edinburgh for his Doctorate. Think, for a moment, of a young man of obviously above average intellect, and no stranger to the rigours of scholarship and intense study, who finishes a bachelor’s degree, plus theological training, plunges immediately into a Master’s degree program which he completes in one year, and then heads across the Atlantic to work on a Doctoral thesis. While in Edinburgh, to finance himself , he took employment as the Chaplain in Saughton Prison. He had to preach every second Sunday and had to visit new inmates just arrived. he could also spend time with older inmates which he did. He even had his own set of keys to doors within the building- except those in Death Row. Furthermore to earn more money he preached (on supply) at various churches in Scotland. It should be noted that he had his wife, Esther with him. He had not wanted to marry her till after he had finished his doctoral program. However, Esther’s parents were wealthy. They agreed to support their daughter while she and Ernest were in Scotland. Hence their marriage and the two of them off to Scotland. They arrived in Scotland September of ‘32. Ernest enrolled in the university and decided, with help from a university tutor, to do his doctoral thesis on The Clapham Sect a group of men who were social reformers.
