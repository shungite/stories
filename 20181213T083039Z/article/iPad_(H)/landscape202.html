When I arrived in Winnipeg in 1919 I made a habit of visiting various churches to determine what part of the city I would live in.I dropped into Westminster one September evening and was delighted to find that a Boys’ Conference was being held and the speaker was Mr. Taylor Staten. He is the man who started Tuxis and Trail Rangers — a great friend of mine. He spotted me in the congregation and told the Conference the story of three lads from Calgary who came to the Lake of The Woods in 1910 to a Boys’ Conference. He related that two of them had given their lives in the First World War, and he had had a visit with the remaining one a few months earlier while lecturing to troops during the march of occupation in Germany; I had obtained leave and had travelled 20 kilometres to listen to my friend. Taylor Staten then asked me to come up to the pulpit and say a few words. Although that was my introduction to Westminster, I really had the Westminster table pinned on me many years before I ever heard of this church. The next evening a group of 12 year old lads called at my room in the Y and invited me to come as their teacher. An auspicious start. Westminster has always had grand ministers and very able associates. When pulpit supply was required, outstanding ministers or laymen fro all corners of the east preached. They included our own church missionary from China and our own Sunday School missionary from India. A large Bible was kept on the pulpit which visitors and preachers signed. Names such as Dr. Grenfell   of Labrador, Ramsey MacDonald, former Prime Minister of Britain, Rev. Professor Moffat (translator of the Bible) and many others signed. Sad to relate vandal stole that historic Bible and it has never been recovered. Times and customs of dress change. On a hot Sunday in June, 1923, I was inducted as an elder. It was usual to wear a dark suit, tie, etc. and white shirt. My suited been to the cleaners and it reeked of gasoline. The new elder next to me kept sniffing during the ceremony. I was warm enough on that hot Sunday, but became more uncomfortable when the minister announced the title of his sermon; he kept repeating it and seemed to look my way each time. The title of the sermon was ‘Christian Fragrance.’
