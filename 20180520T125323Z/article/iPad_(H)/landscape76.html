In fact Bonnell was a man driven by ideas; specifically he was interested not just in the theology of religion per se, but rather more broadly in how religion impacted on the human condition. He was a wonderful story teller, a keen observer of politics, a witness to the destructive impact of poverty on people — the period was of course mid Depression. He was an evangelical preacher but also a moderate one. He was to become a confidant and advisor to Billy Graham but was not a fundamentalist. (Neither, Graham protested, was he!) “When Rome’s youth became debased and enervated (drained of vitality), when regard was lost for men’s honor and women’s purity, when the sanctity of the home was violated, when her literature became cynical and debased, her dominion ended. The moral life of any people rises or falls with the vitality or decay of its religious life.”(J.S. Bonnell) Bonnell’s sermons at Westminster often reflected his concern for social order. He spoke of the “suicide” of the social order; he showed concern for the breakdown of morality which some prominent writers of his time were convinced was happening and should happen; in response to the Depression he agonized over mass unemployment and the human factor implicit in it; he also recognized the social problems associated with alcohol and its use by both adults and youth. On June 19,1932 ( two days after I was born!) Bonnell preached on the topic “What Is Your Master Passion?” In that sermon he analyzed the motives that operate in men’s lives. He then set forth a challenge calling men and women to dedicate their lives to a Passion for human betterment. For the ills of the human social condition Bonnell was convinced that religious faith was the only sure remedy (and the source of the Passion of which he spoke.) Elsewhere in the world people were troubled by the development of Nazism in Germany, Fascism in Italy and Communism in Russia. Many of the world’s governments found the need to increase their armaments and the size of their armies.
