7)   And so many other details, as people have come to me along this journey with new ideas, dusty documents, and engrossing anecdotes. The hobbyist historian, competent and reliable though he or she may be, tends to be relieved from many of the rigours of professional historiography. Doing or “curating” history can be great fun, and the hobbyist relishes that aspect of the activity: casting light on those areas of a subject that interest us most, rather than what the profession demands we research. But no historian, no matter how great, can say or know it all. Herodotus, Gibbon, Toynbee, Marx, Churchill and Foner had something to say, and said it. I doubt they agonized about everything they didn’t say. Westminster has, probably, the largest archive of any church in Winnipeg. If anyone tried to cover all of it — they would fill a very long, boring book. That is not to say that there isn’t anything beyond what I have included that isn’t interesting. There is. I just haven’t included it. — your unrepentant curator, Roy Halstead
