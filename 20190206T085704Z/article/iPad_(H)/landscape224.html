She and Bill Butler were married on October 9, 1977, at their home in NDG, and are the proud parents of Peter, born August 25, 1978, and Catherine, born May 26, 1980. Bill is also a grad of Queen’s (1964) and has an MA from the University of Toronto (1965). He teaches mathematics in a CEGEP in Montreal. Fran went to Queen’s on a provincial scholarship the summer we moved to Winnipeg (1963). She worked one summer (1964) in Winnipeg prior to our family trip overseas, and the other summers did typing and computer work for some professors in the English department at Queen’s. She graduated in honours history and English in 1967, and obtained a teaching position at Wheable Secondary School in London. On June 29, 1968, she and Don Menard (also a ʼ 67 history grad from Queen’s) were married in the chapel of First-St. Andrew’s in London. Following a year of social work in Toronto, Don joined the Laurier Secondary staff in London. Fran was at Wheable until they became the proud parents of David (August 15, 1974) and Beth (December 22, 1976.) Fran has been extremely active in church and community work as well as being available for substitute teaching. Even more important for Dorothy and me personally, Fran keeps in close touch with our “going out” and our “coming in.” She checks our house and our mail and does our banking when we are away. Without her assistance, we would not be able to do our worry-free travelling. Besides being head of history at Westminster Secondary School now, Don is also available for chores at 406 Wortley that are too heavy for an eighty-year-old! Doug did his BA at the University of Winnipeg, and by dint of his work for several summers on the university farm, went to York in 1970. He completed his PhD in psychology, and, following a postdoctoral year at Boulder, Colorado, joined the staff of St. Thomas University in Fredericton. He and Jane Bowden were married in the chapel of Westminster in Winnipeg on August 25, 1972. Jane is from Winnipeg, a graduate of U of T in early childhood education and from the Toronto Royal Conservatory as a clarinetist and now has a large clarinet class. They, too, are proud parents  — Nicholas, May 24, 1979, and Samuel, October 18, 1983.
