Missions: A Brief History Of Incredible Efforts We give money to the United Church of Canada to look after matters missionary for us. We also have what we call A Minute for Mission during church services. We are also personally involved as a Congregation concerned with what we call Outreach. We started a day care program in 1965 that morphed into a full Day Care Centre. Under Charles Huband and friends we started something called Westminster Housing which then created a Board of its own and now operates some distance from us working to provide affordable, quality house to people with limited means. Westminster congregant, Phyllis Reader gathered some Westminster friends together and the organization, International Hope, was born, supplying hospital furniture and supplies to distant countries. It is still supported by loyal volunteers from this congregation. (Phyllis at right) Marilyn Huband started a ‘Mothers’ Morning Off’ program with Westminster volunteers to reach out to women in the Wolseley community. This had been a program operating across the City. We opted in. At Christmas time we focus attention on our neighbourhood elementary school with gifts of mittens and other warm winter garments for children whose parents are often too strapped for money to buy these things. We also donate to an organization called L.I.T.E. This program supports initiatives by aboriginal people in Winnipeg to set up self-sustaining businesses. Through encouragement from our former minister, Greg Glatz, Westminster volunteers shoulder responsibility for The Bell Tower Cafe, a means of distributing food hampers to people in the Wolseley area as well as providing free food and entertainment to the local community every second Friday. Bell Tower has its own Board headed by Meagan Pauls, as Coordinator with solid backing from the Congregation.
