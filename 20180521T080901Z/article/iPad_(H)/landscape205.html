Subdivisions like Silver Heights, Kirkfield Park, and Windsor Park were only names. Oxford, Westworth, and Trinity Churches were nonexistent. Transcona was a separate town and we had only a smattering of members coming from what Larry Zolf calls “Winnipeg’s nort’end.” What a change we witnessed (and were part of) in our second tour of duty at Westminster! I remember well that day early in July 1963, as we drove into Winnipeg from the west: homes and stores and schools and churches right out to Kirkfield Park; there were apartment houses (albeit mostly three-storey walk-ups); Polo Park shopping mall already established at Portage and St. James; and hard by the St. James bridge overpass, the spanking new multi-storey Kiltarton Towers. Soon, we were to discover “Winnipeg’s finest” — the Edinburgh House on Wellington Crescent, and, as they say in almost every advertisement, “much, much more.” In a sense we were not fully prepared for the traumatic change at Westminster. Before we left Lakeview we had canvassed the possibility of having a seniors’ club, but there were so few members over sixty-five or seventy that such a club was considered neither necessary nor viable. In contrast, one of the most active groups at Westminster was the “Sunshine Club,” sixty or seventy-five seniors who met every Friday afternoon, played cards, put on their own programs, and danced to the music of their own orchestra. Their Christmas turkey dinner and June picnic swelled their numbers to over one hundred. Doris Marshall was the staff person in charge of senior programming, and later (because she was a trained deaconess) added Christian education to her busy portfolio and gave excellent service to Westminster. By December, Mack MacIver, just retired from the air force chaplaincy with the rank of wing commander, joined our staff on a three-quarter time basis. A most affable Cape Bretoner, Mack was with us for five happy years. Following a very lengthy and debilitating illness with multiple sclerosis, his wife, Charlotte, died. Mack remarried, and he and Myrtle remain two of our very good Winnipeg friends.
