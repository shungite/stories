Although that was my introduction to Westminster, I really had the Westminster table pinned on me many years before I ever heard of this church. The next evening a group of 12 year old lads called at my room in the Y and invited me to come as their teacher. An auspicious start. Westminster has always had grand ministers and very able associates. When pulpit supply was required, outstanding ministers or laymen fro all corners of the east preached. They included our own church missionary from China and our own Sunday School missionary from India. A large Bible was kept on the pulpit which visitors and preachers signed. Names such as Dr. Grenfell   of Labrador, Ramsey MacDonald, former Prime Minister of Britain, Rev. Professor Moffat (translator of the Bible) and many others signed. Sad to relate vandal stole that historic Bible and it has never been recovered. Times and customs of dress change. On a hot Sunday in June, 1923, I was inducted as an elder. It was usual to wear a dark suit, tie, etc. and white shirt. My suited been to the cleaners and it reeked of gasoline. The new elder next to me kept sniffing during the ceremony. I was warm enough on that hot Sunday, but became more uncomfortable when the minister announced the title of his sermon; he kept repeating it and seemed to look my way each time. The title of the sermon was ‘Christian Fragrance.’ One event each year was the congregational picnic at the parks. One year we hired a C.N.R. four or five coach train, sold tickets all over Winnipeg and spent the Saturday afternoon at Victoria Beach. They gave me $200.00 to buy prizes and we spent it all. When the races started every person or stranger at Victoria Beach turned up to get in on the races and the grand prize list. Senator John T. Haig acted as our policeman and saw to it that no-one took part unless they were connected with Westminster.
