Rev. Nelson R. Mercer Rev. Nelson R. Mercer, B.A., D.D. was our seventh minister serving from 1955 to 1962. He then moved to Calgary and spent the remainder of his career in Alberta. The Curator of this eBook is embarrassed to admit that we seem to have no information about Nelson, his life or his ministry. He was a fine man and was well respected by the congregation. But we should know more, much more about a person who was our leader for seven years. If you, reading this paragraph, can give us either information or leads to information on Nelson we would be most grateful. Please contact our church office. R.D.   — In His Own Words R.D. (Bert) Macfarlane represents a significant portion of the congregation which has always been the back bone of a successful Church — dedicated volunteers; the tireless men and women who run our programs and shoulder responsibilities for — among many reasons — the joy of serving. R.D. worked  in our Sunday School and our Athletic program for young people. He edited a newsletter for many years. He also fathered a girl who became, like himself, a leader every bit as strong as her father; Marilyn Macfarlane-Huband. Bert can tell his own story. The Annual Meeting he alludes to in the beginning took place in 1972 during the Ministry of Reid Vipond, the same year this document was written … “At the Annual Meeting of Westminster held in the Sanctuary this year Dr. Vipond was kind enough to draw attention to the fact that I had completed 53 years of activity in this church and invited the congregation to give suitable recognition. I am deeply grateful to our minister for making this reference. Fifty three years is a short period in life’s record and I have enjoyed every bit of it and am thankful that I had the opportunity of being associated with such fine people in serving the Master.
