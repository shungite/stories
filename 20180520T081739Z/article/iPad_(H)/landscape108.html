To his surprise he was quickly replaced and was then relocated to Northern Saskatchewan, a mission field with four preaching points. It was summer and he worked there till he was ready in the fall to return to college. His plan was to enrol at Victoria College in Toronto. However, he received word that his father was very ill. So he opted instead to enrol at Mt. Allison in New Brunswick to be closer to family.Then he faced a mind change. Mt.Allison had been a Methodist College and Ernest had been a Methodist. But the United Church now existed and he decided to turn his back on methodism and seek a new experience at what had been a Presbyterian college. He turned to Pine Hills Theological College near Halifax, “an institution where no Newfoundland probationer had ever before gone. ” An advantage to enrolling in Pine Hills was that it was affiliated with Dalhousie University, the one for Theology the other for general studies. He arrived at Pine Hills and was interviewed by Principal McKinnon, who had been our (Westminster’s) second minister. “I took a taxi, drove up to Pine Hills and asked to see Principal Clarence McKinnon. Principal McKinnon was an extraordinary man.He was among the most influential teachers I have ever met, and certainly one of those whom I would most feely call my friend. He was not an original scholar but I suspect there were few scholars of his time who could match him, as in church history class he could touch dead things of the past and bring them to life. More important than anything academic was the immeasurable impact of his character. Critics at the time used to say that all Pine Hill students became little imitations of Clarence McKinnon. Ernest was able to get his BA in 3 years and in five years both his B.A, from Dalhousie and the honours Diploma from Pine Hill. He graduated in Arts in 1929 and in Theology in 1931. One further look at Ernest’s summer ‘jobs.’ In 1928 he was assigned as minister to Piapot, Saskatchewan (downtown Piapot pictured overleaf), west of Swift Current. His transportation in the area was again by horse. The owner of the grocery store, a fellow by the name of A.J. Mercer decided to play a trick on the young minister.
