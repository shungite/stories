Following the phenomenal growth of the Sunday School other organizations commenced a successful run of operations. Youth and Men’s clubs became active — the enthusiastic Bible class of Mr. John A. McAulay, Stan Jones and the Actimist Club — the large enrolment of a public speaking men’s club gathered from all over this city, drew  at one time 3000 listeners attending a session in the Sanctuary , the halls of the church and the Tivioli Theatre (across the street) — a selected group of young men who spoke in churches in Brandon, Neepawa, Gladstone and other places. It has been my privilege to represent Westminster at Presbytery, Manitoba Conference, The World Congress of Sunday School  Workers held in Toronto (where we had a delegation of seven), at A.O.T.S. conventions in Vancouver, Banff, Kingston and Halifax which we also enjoyed, and we have had a busy, happy time. We have benefitted greatly in having the opportunity of association with the fine people of Westminster, and we have been proud of our church wherever visits were made. Looking backward the outstanding things in all the years have been the association with  the Boys’ Department for many years, and later the 15 years editing the Westminster Newsletter. This church is ALIVE! Attend as an observer at one of the 44 member Board meetings gathered around a square and where the chairmen have done lots of homework in preparing their arguments   and briefs. This is not a ‘Yes Men’ organization. Matters of Westminster are thoroughly discussed and agreed upon. I welcomed the opportunity of the Newsletter Editor to write down a few thoughts of the past years. All these years have been a family team effort. My wife and I still enjoy our membership and are happy to realize that our daughter, Marilyn, has taken up the reins.” R.D.
