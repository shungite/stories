Presbytery acceded to their request. They rented Victoria Hall on Notre Dame for their services till they had time to build a new church building. All that in 1892. On 1 January 1893 their first service was held. Professor Baird from the Manitoba College conducted the service with attendance of 200 people. Eleven days later a small group met and chose the name Westminster. Shortly thereafter they sent a call to Rev. Charles Bruce Pitblado who had been their minister at St. Andrew’s and had gone to live in California for his health. Pitblado accepted the call and so we began our 125 years of service to the community up to this day. Following are pictures and short biographical notes on some of those founders of our congregation. We regret that there are so few pictures of the original group and so little information about them. In their day it’s doubtful that they thought of anyone 125 years after them thinking to know who they really were. Whatever they thought, we thank them for establishing one of the finest congregations with which this city has been blest.
