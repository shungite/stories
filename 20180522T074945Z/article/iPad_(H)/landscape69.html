Stories of Westminster United Church & its People / Page 69
A Kind of Post Script As mentioned earlier the congregation had 4 separate Mission Societies. Beyond those there was a Ladies Society, the Westminster Men’s Club, the Young Men’s Club, the Westminster Church Tennis Club, and a Badminton Club. Add to these The Sewing Circle and the Church Choir under Herb Sadler. And besides our morning and evening services every Sunday there was a regular Midweek Prayer Meeting (Wednesday nights). And don’t forget the Tuxis Boys and the CGIT which met weekly. For a congregation of over 1300 people there were an amazing number of inducements all week long to engage and hold people’s interest in their church. But then … There was nothing to engage people’s attention and interest outside the church compared with what we have today. The was no Television. However, in 1929 movies with sound were a year old (thanks to Walt Disney). The Osborne Stadium (located across Osborne from The Legislative buildings) would be built in 1932. (The Winnipeg Blue Bombers which played there wouldn’t exist till 1935.) On the other hand organized sports generally, across Canada, were not commanding the attention they get today. Outlets for alcoholic beverages were not as thick on the ground as they are today and, in any event, women drank at home. There was ice skating (circa 1920) where the University of Winnipeg Chemistry building is now located. People skated to the music of a shivering twelve-man band. The building didn’t finally become a roller rink till the mid 1930s. (The wood floor of the rink became the feature wall in the atrium of the Chemistry Building.) The Bulletin, that June 2nd Sunday, 1929, also called attention to a concert the next day, in Young Methodist Church in aid of the Gimli Fresh Air Camp (admission 25¢). On Tuesday afternoon our Mission band was holding a Tea at 3:30, and at 6:00 PM our Pitblado Missionary Auxillary was holding a Picnic in Assiniboine Park. On Wednesday, people were advised, The Manitoba College Auxillary was hiding its Annual Dinner (50¢). Church was the social and activity centre for many of the congregation. And the Sanctuary was full on Sunday morning.
