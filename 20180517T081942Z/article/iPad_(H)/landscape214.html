There were at least two important decisions regarding property and finances made by the trustees and board at Westminster in our time there. First, the old manse at 120 Maryland Street was being used by Manitoba conference as the office for the conference staff and the resource centre. (141 Waterloo had been bought as a manse in about 1958.) After fairly lengthy negotiations with the conference executive, Westminster agreed to tear down the old manse and build a two-storey office building to serve the conference needs. This was accomplished in 1964. The conference rent is used to pay off the $80,000 mortgage on the office building, so it has been a win-win situation for the church. The mortgage is just about paid off at this point (1992), and from now on, the conference rental will become church income for Westminster. Second, about 1965 I suggested that we should set up a foundation fund for the church. There were always people asking if there was some particular need toward which a memorial contribution could be made.
