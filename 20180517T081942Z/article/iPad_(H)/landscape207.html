In our years, Glen Harrison directed many special choir recitals, and on numerous occasions had a half dozen Winnipeg Symphony players for orchestral accompaniment. Don Menzies, as well as guest organists, have played (and broadcast) many recitals. I have heard both Marcel Dupré and E. Power Biggs in recital on that outstanding instrument. Writing this much about staff, I’ll also add a word here about our custodians. When we were first at Westminster, Mr. Jake Flipse looked after the church. A native of Holland, he had worked with a landscape gardening company when he first came to Canada. I’m not sure when he came to the church, but Mr. and Mrs. Flipse occupied the three-room apartment on the fourth floor of the church hall for many years. Mr. Flipse was quiet, unassuming, very kind, very efficient, and, of course, extremely capable in terms of the church lawn. His hours were long; he was literally on call anytime, all the time, from when the doors opened in the morning until the last meeting was over at night. He had Monday evening and all day Tuesday off. His replacement for those times was old Mr. Fish. I was glad he was there, because it made possible my little joke: ours was the only church in town that employed Fish and Flipse! Upon returning to Westminster in 1963, we still had a Dutch custodian, Rinke Zielstra. Mrs. Zielstra helped a good deal, especially in Rinke’s last years when his health was failing. They were succeeded by Mr. and Mrs. Abe Thiessen, who in a year or two, became fed up with the sixty-eight steps to the fourth floor, and moved into their own home nearby. They also pointed out how very little private life they had by living in the building where they worked. In the church office, Mrs. Violet Ridd had been secretary for eight or ten years before our arrival. However, a few months into my tenure she suddenly resigned. Mrs. Anna Burt was hired to replace her, and for many years she served the church with great efficiency. She had been a Scottish war bride, coming to Canada shortly after the war to marry Phil whom she had met when they were both in the Armed Forces. They had two daughters, Margaret and Anne. Phil was an orderly at the Deer Lodge Veterans Hospital. Mrs. Burt was extremely efficient, and in short order had the office routine down to a science.
