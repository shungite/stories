We asked him wherefore  his interest in this organization. In his own words … My original Loyalist ancestor, Robert Campbell, served as a Sergeant Major with Butler’s Rangers, a militia allied with the British. They conducted raids throughout upstate New York and Pennsylvania seriously harassing the patriot forces and causing a lot of panic. He came to Grantham Township, which is now part of St. Catharines, in 1783 when the British failed to get amnesty for Loyalists and restitution of their property included in the Treaty of Paris which ended the conflict. We don't know exactly where he had farmed prior to the revolution, but it was very likely in the Mohawk Valley in upstate New York from whence Colonel Butler recruited the majority of his rangers. At the time of writing, Robert is Minister of McClure United Church on Greenwood Place, south of Portage Ave. He is also thinking long thought about retirement.
